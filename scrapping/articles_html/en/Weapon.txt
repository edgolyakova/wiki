<p>A <b>weapon</b>, <b>arm</b> or <b>armament</b> is any implement or device that can be used to inflict physical damage, harm, or kill. Weapons are used to increase the efficacy and efficiency of activities such as <a href="/wiki/Hunting" title="Hunting">hunting</a>, <a href="/wiki/Crime" title="Crime">crime</a>, <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement" title="Law enforcement">law enforcement</a>, <a href="/wiki/Self-defense" title="Self-defense">self-defense</a>, <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">warfare</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">suicide</a>. In broader context, weapons may be construed to include anything used to gain a tactical, strategic, material or mental advantage over an adversary or enemy target.
</p><p>While ordinary objects – sticks, rocks, bottles, chairs, vehicles – can be <a href="/wiki/Improvised_weapon" title="Improvised weapon">used as weapons</a>, many objects are expressly designed for the purpose; these range from simple implements such as <a href="/wiki/Club_(weapon)" title="Club (weapon)">clubs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Axe" title="Axe">axes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sword" title="Sword">swords</a>, to complicated modern <a href="/wiki/Firearm" title="Firearm">firearms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tank" title="Tank">tanks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Intercontinental_ballistic_missile" title="Intercontinental ballistic missile">intercontinental ballistic missiles</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biological_warfare" title="Biological warfare">biological weapons</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cyberweapon" title="Cyberweapon">cyberweapons</a>. Something that has been re-purposed, converted, or enhanced to become a weapon of war is termed <b>weaponized</b>, such as a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weaponized_virus" title="Weaponized virus">weaponized virus</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weaponized_lasers" title="Weaponized lasers">weaponized laser</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_weapons" title="History of weapons">History of weapons</a></div>
<p>The use of weapons is a major driver of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cultural_Evolution" title="Cultural Evolution">cultural evolution</a> and <a href="/wiki/History" title="History">human history</a> up to today, since weapons are a type of tool which is used to dominate and subdue autonomous agents such as animals and by that allow for an expansion of the cultural niche, while simultaneously other weapon users (i.e., agents such as humans, groups, cultures) are able to adapt to weapons of enemies by learning, triggering a continuous process of competitive technological, skill and cognitive improvement (<a href="/wiki/Arms_race" title="Arms race">arms race</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Prehistoric">Prehistoric</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Prehistoric">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:N%C3%A9olithique_0001.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1704" data-file-width="2560" decoding="async" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/N%C3%A9olithique_0001.jpg/220px-N%C3%A9olithique_0001.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/N%C3%A9olithique_0001.jpg/330px-N%C3%A9olithique_0001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/N%C3%A9olithique_0001.jpg/440px-N%C3%A9olithique_0001.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:N%C3%A9olithique_0001.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An array of <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">Neolithic</a> artifacts, including <a href="/wiki/Bracelet" title="Bracelet">bracelets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Axe" title="Axe">axe</a> heads, <a href="/wiki/Chisel" title="Chisel">chisels</a>, and polishing tools.</div></div></div>
<p>The use of objects as weapons has been observed among <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Common_Chimpanzee" title="Common Chimpanzee">chimpanzees</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pruetz1_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pruetz1-2">[2]</a></sup> leading to speculation that early <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hominids" title="Hominids">hominids</a> used weapons as early as five million years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> However, this can not be confirmed using physical evidence because wooden clubs, spears, and unshaped stones would have left an ambiguous record. The earliest unambiguous weapons to be found are the <a href="/wiki/Sch%C3%B6ningen_spears" title="Schöningen spears">Schöningen spears</a>, eight wooden throwing spears dating back more than 300,000 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> At the site of Nataruk in Turkana, Kenya, numerous human skeletons dating to 10,000 years ago may present evidence of traumatic injuries to the head, neck, ribs, knees and hands, including <a href="/wiki/Obsidian" title="Obsidian">obsidian</a> projectiles embedded in the bones that might have been caused from arrows and clubs during conflict between two hunter-gatherer groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> But the evidence interpretation of warfare at Nataruk has been challenged.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ancient_history">Ancient history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Ancient history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ballista-quadrirotis.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2104" data-file-width="3212" decoding="async" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Ballista-quadrirotis.jpeg/220px-Ballista-quadrirotis.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Ballista-quadrirotis.jpeg/330px-Ballista-quadrirotis.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Ballista-quadrirotis.jpeg/440px-Ballista-quadrirotis.jpeg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Ballista-quadrirotis.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A four-wheeled <a href="/wiki/Ballista" title="Ballista">ballista</a> drawn by armored <a href="/wiki/Cataphract" title="Cataphract">cataphract</a> horses, <abbr title="circa">c.</abbr><span style="white-space:nowrap;"> 400</span>.</div></div></div>
<p>The earliest <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_ancient_weapons" title="List of ancient weapons">ancient weapons</a> were evolutionary improvements of late <a href="/wiki/Neolithic" title="Neolithic">neolithic</a> implements, but significant improvements in materials and crafting techniques led to a series of revolutions in <a href="/wiki/Military_technology" title="Military technology">military technology</a>.
</p><p>The development of metal tools began with <a href="/wiki/Copper" title="Copper">copper</a> during the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copper_Age" title="Copper Age">Copper Age</a> (about 3,300 BC) and was followed by the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a>, leading to the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age_sword" title="Bronze Age sword">Bronze Age sword</a> and similar weapons.
</p><p>During the Bronze Age, the first defensive structures and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fortifications" title="Fortifications">fortifications</a> appeared as well,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ashow-siegecraft_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-ashow-siegecraft-11">[11]</a></sup> indicating an increased need for security. Weapons designed to breach fortifications followed soon after, such as the <a href="/wiki/Battering_ram" title="Battering ram">battering ram</a>, which was in use by 2500 BC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ashow-siegecraft_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-ashow-siegecraft-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p><p>The development of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_ferrous_metallurgy" title="History of ferrous metallurgy">iron-working</a> around 1300 BC in Greece had an important impact on the development of ancient weapons. It was not the introduction of early <a href="/wiki/Iron_Age_sword" title="Iron Age sword">Iron Age swords</a>, however, as they were not superior to their bronze predecessors,
but rather the <a href="/wiki/Domestication_of_the_horse" title="Domestication of the horse">domestication of the horse</a> and widespread use of <a href="/wiki/Spoke" title="Spoke">spoked</a> wheels by c. 2000 BC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> This led to the creation of the light, horse-drawn <a href="/wiki/Chariot" title="Chariot">chariot</a>, whose improved mobility proved important during this era.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whedbee_2005_p._50_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-Whedbee_2005_p._50-13">[13]</a></sup> Spoke-wheeled chariot usage peaked around 1300 BC and then declined, ceasing to be militarily relevant by the 4th century BC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Cavalry" title="Cavalry">Cavalry</a> developed once horses were bred to support the weight of a human.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> The horse extended the range and increased the speed of attacks.
</p><p>In addition to land based weaponry, <a href="/wiki/Warship" title="Warship">warships</a>, such as the <a href="/wiki/Trireme" title="Trireme">trireme</a>, were in use by the 7th century BC.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>European warfare during the <a href="/wiki/Post-classical_history" title="Post-classical history">Post-classical history</a> was dominated by elite groups of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knights" title="Knights">knights</a> supported by massed <a href="/wiki/Infantry" title="Infantry">infantry</a> (both in combat and ranged roles). They were involved in mobile combat and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sieges" title="Sieges">sieges</a> which involved various <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siege_weapon" title="Siege weapon">siege weapons</a> and tactics. Knights on horseback developed tactics for charging with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lances" title="Lances">lances</a> providing an impact on the enemy formations and then drawing more practical weapons (such as <a href="/wiki/Sword" title="Sword">swords</a>) once they entered into the melee. By contrast, infantry, in the age before structured formations, relied on cheap, sturdy weapons such as <a href="/wiki/Spear" title="Spear">spears</a> and billhooks in close combat and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bow_(weapon)" title="Bow (weapon)">bows</a> from a distance. As armies became more professional, their equipment was standardized and infantry transitioned to <a href="/wiki/Pike_(weapon)" title="Pike (weapon)">pikes</a>. Pikes are normally seven to eight feet in length, and used in conjunction with smaller side-arms (short sword).
</p><p>In Eastern and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Middle_Eastern" title="Middle Eastern">Middle Eastern</a> warfare, similar tactics were developed independent of European influences.
</p><p>The introduction of <a href="/wiki/Gunpowder" title="Gunpowder">gunpowder</a> from the Asia at the end of this period revolutionized warfare. Formations of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Musketeers" title="Musketeers">musketeers</a>, protected by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pikemen" title="Pikemen">pikemen</a> came to dominate open battles, and the <a href="/wiki/Cannon" title="Cannon">cannon</a> replaced the <a href="/wiki/Trebuchet" title="Trebuchet">trebuchet</a> as the dominant <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siege_weapon" title="Siege weapon">siege weapon</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_history">Modern history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Modern history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1097763485" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="box-More_citations_needed_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div class="mbox-image-div"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg"><img alt="" data-file-height="399" data-file-width="512" decoding="async" height="39" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" width="50"/></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Referencing_for_beginners" title="Help:Referencing for beginners">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_modern">Early modern</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Early modern">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The European <a href="/wiki/Renaissance" title="Renaissance">Renaissance</a> marked the beginning of the implementation of firearms in western warfare. <a href="/wiki/Gun" title="Gun">Guns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rocket" title="Rocket">rockets</a> were introduced to the battlefield.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Firearm" title="Firearm">Firearms</a> are qualitatively different from earlier weapons because they release energy from combustible <a href="/wiki/Propellant" title="Propellant">propellants</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Gunpowder" title="Gunpowder">gunpowder</a>, rather than from a counter-weight or spring. This energy is released very rapidly and can be replicated without much effort by the user. Therefore even early firearms such as the <a href="/wiki/Arquebus" title="Arquebus">arquebus</a> were much more <a href="/wiki/Power_(physics)" title="Power (physics)">powerful</a> than human-powered weapons. Firearms became increasingly important and effective during the 16th century to 19th century, with progressive improvements in <a href="/wiki/Lock_(firearm)" title="Lock (firearm)">ignition mechanisms</a> followed by revolutionary changes in <a href="/wiki/Ammunition" title="Ammunition">ammunition</a> handling and propellant. During the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Civil_War" title="U.S. Civil War">U.S. Civil War</a> new applications of firearms including the <a href="/wiki/Machine_gun" title="Machine gun">machine gun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ironclad_warship" title="Ironclad warship">ironclad warship</a> emerged that would still be recognizable and useful military weapons today, particularly in <a href="/wiki/Limited_war" title="Limited war">limited conflicts</a>. In the 19th century <a href="/wiki/Warship" title="Warship">warship</a> propulsion changed from <a href="/wiki/Sail" title="Sail">sail</a> power to <a href="/wiki/Fossil_fuel" title="Fossil fuel">fossil fuel</a>-powered <a href="/wiki/Steam_engine" title="Steam engine">steam engines</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Prussian_bayonet_clean.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="93" data-file-width="562" decoding="async" height="50" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Prussian_bayonet_clean.jpg/300px-Prussian_bayonet_clean.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Prussian_bayonet_clean.jpg/450px-Prussian_bayonet_clean.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/7/75/Prussian_bayonet_clean.jpg 2x" width="300"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Prussian_bayonet_clean.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Bayonet" title="Bayonet">bayonet</a> is used as both <a href="/wiki/Knife" title="Knife">knife</a> and, when attached to a rifle, a <a href="/wiki/Polearm" title="Polearm">polearm</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Since the mid-18th century North American French-Indian war through the beginning of the 20th century, human-powered weapons were reduced from the primary weaponry of the battlefield yielding to gunpowder-based weaponry. Sometimes referred to as the "Age of Rifles",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> this period was characterized by the development of firearms for infantry and cannons for support, as well as the beginnings of mechanized weapons such as the <a href="/wiki/Machine_gun" title="Machine gun">machine gun</a>.
Of particular note, <a href="/wiki/Howitzer" title="Howitzer">Howitzers</a> were able to destroy masonry fortresses and other fortifications, and this single invention caused a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Revolution_in_Military_Affairs" title="Revolution in Military Affairs">Revolution in Military Affairs</a> (RMA), establishing tactics and doctrine that are still in use today. <i>See <a href="/wiki/Technology_during_World_War_I" title="Technology during World War I">Technology during World War I</a> for a detailed discussion.</i>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_age">Industrial age</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Industrial age">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vickers_machine_gun_in_the_Battle_of_Passchendaele_-_September_1917.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2480" data-file-width="3433" decoding="async" height="202" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Vickers_machine_gun_in_the_Battle_of_Passchendaele_-_September_1917.jpg/280px-Vickers_machine_gun_in_the_Battle_of_Passchendaele_-_September_1917.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Vickers_machine_gun_in_the_Battle_of_Passchendaele_-_September_1917.jpg/420px-Vickers_machine_gun_in_the_Battle_of_Passchendaele_-_September_1917.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Vickers_machine_gun_in_the_Battle_of_Passchendaele_-_September_1917.jpg/560px-Vickers_machine_gun_in_the_Battle_of_Passchendaele_-_September_1917.jpg 2x" width="280"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vickers_machine_gun_in_the_Battle_of_Passchendaele_-_September_1917.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Vickers_machine_gun" title="Vickers machine gun">Vickers</a> was the successor to the <a href="/wiki/Maxim_gun" title="Maxim gun">Maxim gun</a> and remained in British military service for 79 consecutive years.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cz805.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1119" data-file-width="2370" decoding="async" height="132" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Cz805.png/280px-Cz805.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Cz805.png/420px-Cz805.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Cz805.png/560px-Cz805.png 2x" width="280"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cz805.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Assault_rifle" title="Assault rifle">Assault rifle</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CZ-805_BREN" title="CZ-805 BREN">CZ-805 BREN</a> (produced in Czech Republic).</div></div></div>
<p>An important feature of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrial_age" title="Industrial age">industrial age</a> warfare was <a href="/wiki/Technological_escalation" title="Technological escalation">technological escalation</a> – innovations were rapidly matched through replication or countered by another innovation. The <a href="/wiki/Technology_during_World_War_I" title="Technology during World War I">technological escalation during World War I</a> was profound, including the wide introduction of <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a> into <a href="/wiki/Technology_during_World_War_I" title="Technology during World War I">warfare</a>, and naval warfare with the introduction of <a href="/wiki/Aircraft_carrier" title="Aircraft carrier">aircraft carriers</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This paragraph needs references to reliable sources. (December 2021)">citations needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_I">World War I</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: World War I">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> marked the entry of fully industrialized warfare as well as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weapons_of_mass_destruction" title="Weapons of mass destruction">weapons of mass destruction</a> (<i>e.g.</i>, <a href="/wiki/Chemical_weapon" title="Chemical weapon">chemical</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biological_weapons" title="Biological weapons">biological weapons</a>), and new weapons were developed quickly to meet wartime needs. Above all, it promised to the military commanders the independence from the horse and the resurgence in <a href="/wiki/Maneuver_warfare" title="Maneuver warfare">maneuver warfare</a> through extensive use of motor vehicles. The changes that these <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_technology_during_World_War_II" title="Military technology during World War II">military technologies</a> underwent before and during the <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">Second World War</a> were evolutionary, but defined the development for the rest of the century.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This paragraph needs references to reliable sources. (December 2021)">citations needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Interwar">Interwar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Interwar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This period of innovation in weapon design continued in the inter-war period (between WW I and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Technological_escalation_during_World_War_II" title="Technological escalation during World War II">WW II</a>) with continuous evolution of weapon systems by all major industrial powers.  The major armament firms were the <a href="/wiki/Schneider-Creusot" title="Schneider-Creusot">Schneider-Creusot</a> (based in France), the <a href="/wiki/%C5%A0koda_Works" title="Škoda Works">Škoda Works</a> (Czechoslovakia), and <a href="/wiki/Vickers" title="Vickers">Vickers</a> (Great Britain). The 1920s were committed to disarmament and outlawing of war and poison gas, but rearmament picked up rapidly in the 1930s. The munitions makers responded nimbly to the rapidly shifting strategic and economic landscape. The main purchasers of munitions from the big three companies were Romania, Yugoslavia, Greece, and Turkey—and, to a lesser extent, in Poland, Finland, the Baltic States, and the Soviet Union.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Criminalizing_poison_gas">Criminalizing poison gas</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Criminalizing poison gas">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>Realistic critics understood that war could not really be outlawed, but its worst excesses might be banned. Poison gas became the focus of a worldwide crusade in the 1920s.  Poison gas did not win battles, and the generals did not want it. The soldiers hated it far more intensely than bullets or explosive shells. By 1918, chemical shells made up 35 per cent of French ammunition supplies, 25 per cent of British, and 20 per cent of the American stock. The “Protocol for the Prohibition of the Use in War of Asphyxiating, Poisonous, or Other Gases and of Bacteriological Methods of Warfare” ["Geneva Protocol"] was issued in 1925, and was accepted as policy by all major countries. In 1937 poison gas was manufactured in large quantities, but not used except against nations that lacked modern weapons or gas masks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Many modern military weapons, particularly ground-based ones, are relatively minor improvements of weapon systems developed during <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. <i>See <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_technology_during_World_War_II" title="Military technology during World War II">military technology during World War II</a> for a detailed discussion.</i>
<a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> however, perhaps marked the most frantic period of weapons development in the history of humanity. Massive numbers of new designs and concepts were fielded, and all existing technologies were improved between 1939 and 1945. The most powerful weapon invented during this period was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atomic_bomb" title="Atomic bomb">atomic bomb</a>, however many other weapons influenced the world, such as jet planes and radar, but were overshadowed by the visibility of nuclear weapons and long-range rockets.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This paragraph needs references to reliable sources. (December 2021)">citations needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Nuclear_weapons">Nuclear weapons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Nuclear weapons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_holocaust" title="Nuclear holocaust">Nuclear holocaust</a></div>
<p>Since the realization of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mutually_assured_destruction" title="Mutually assured destruction">mutually assured destruction</a> (MAD), the nuclear option of all-out war is no longer considered a survivable scenario.  During the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a> in the years following <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, both the United States and the Soviet Union engaged in a <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_arms_race" title="Nuclear arms race">nuclear arms race</a>. Each country and their allies continually attempted to out-develop each other in the field of nuclear armaments. Once the joint technological capabilities reached the point of being able to ensure the destruction of the Earth x100 fold, then a new tactic had to be developed.  With this realization, armaments development funding shifted back to primarily sponsoring the development of conventional arms technologies for support of <a href="/wiki/Limited_war" title="Limited war">limited wars</a> rather than <a href="/wiki/Total_war" title="Total war">total war</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NuclearDefunding_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-NuclearDefunding-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Types">Types</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Types">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_user">By user</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: By user">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl><dd><i>– what person or unit uses the weapon</i></dd></dl>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Personal_weapon" title="Personal weapon">Personal weapons</a> (or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Small_arms" title="Small arms">small arms</a>) – designed to be used by a single person.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SALW" title="SALW">Light weapons</a> – 'man-portable' weapons that may require a small team to operate.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup></li>
<li>Heavy weapons – <a href="/wiki/Artillery" title="Artillery">artillery</a> and similar weapons larger than light weapons (see <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SALW" title="SALW">SALW</a>).</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crew_served_weapon" title="Crew served weapon">Crew served weapons</a> – larger than personal weapons, requiring two or more people to operate correctly.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Category:Fortification_weapons" title="Category:Fortification weapons">Fortification weapons</a> – mounted in a permanent installation, or used primarily within a fortification.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mountain_gun" title="Mountain gun">Mountain weapons</a> – for use by mountain forces or those operating in difficult terrain.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Category:Vehicle_weapons" title="Category:Vehicle weapons">Vehicle weapons</a> – to be mounted on any type of <a href="/wiki/Combat_vehicle" title="Combat vehicle">combat vehicle</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Railway_weapon" title="Railway weapon">Railway weapons</a> – designed to be mounted on railway cars, including armored trains.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/List_of_aircraft_weapons" title="List of aircraft weapons">Aircraft weapons</a> – carried on and used by some type of <a href="/wiki/Aircraft" title="Aircraft">aircraft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a>, or other aerial vehicle.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Category:Naval_weapons" title="Category:Naval weapons">Naval weapons</a> – mounted on <a href="/wiki/Ship" title="Ship">ships</a> and <a href="/wiki/Submarine" title="Submarine">submarines</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Space_weapon" title="Space weapon">Space weapons</a> – are designed to be used in or launched from space.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autonomous_weapon" title="Autonomous weapon">Autonomous weapons</a> – are capable of accomplishing a mission with limited or no human intervention.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_function">By function</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: By function">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl><dd><i>– the construction of the weapon and principle of operation</i></dd></dl>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Antimatter_weapon" title="Antimatter weapon">Antimatter weapons</a> (theoretical) would combine matter and <a href="/wiki/Antimatter" title="Antimatter">antimatter</a> to cause a powerful explosion.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Archery" title="Archery">Archery</a> weapons operate by using a tensioned string and bent solid to launch a projectile.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Artillery" title="Artillery">Artillery</a> are firearms capable of launching heavy projectiles over long distances.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Biological_weapon" title="Biological weapon">Biological weapons</a> spread biological agents, causing disease or infection.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Chemical_weapon" title="Chemical weapon">Chemical weapons</a>, poisoning and causing reactions.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Directed-energy_weapon" title="Directed-energy weapon">Energy weapons</a> rely on concentrating forms of energy to attack, such as lasers or sonic attack.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Explosive_weapon" title="Explosive weapon">Explosive weapons</a> use a physical explosion to create blast concussion or spread <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shrapnel_(fragment)" title="Shrapnel (fragment)">shrapnel</a>.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Firearms" title="Firearms">Firearms</a> use a chemical charge to launch projectiles.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Improvised_weapon" title="Improvised weapon">Improvised weapons</a> are common objects, reused as weapons, such as crowbars and kitchen knives.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Incendiary_weapon" title="Incendiary weapon">Incendiary weapons</a> cause damage by fire.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Non-lethal_weapon" title="Non-lethal weapon">Non-lethal weapons</a> are designed to subdue without killing.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Magnetic_weapon" title="Magnetic weapon">Magnetic weapons</a> use magnetic fields to propel projectiles, or to focus particle beams.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Melee_weapon" title="Melee weapon">Melee weapons</a> operate as physical extensions of the user's body and directly impact a close target.
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blade_weapon" title="Blade weapon">Blade weapons</a>, designed to pierce through flesh and cause bleeding.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Blunt_instrument" title="Blunt instrument">Blunt instruments</a>, designed to break bones, concuss or produce <a href="/wiki/Crush_injury" title="Crush injury">crush injuries</a>.</li></ul></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Missile" title="Missile">Missiles</a> are rockets which are guided to their target after launch. (Also a general term for <a href="/wiki/Projectile" title="Projectile">projectile</a> weapons).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Loitering_munition" title="Loitering munition">Loitering munitions</a>, designed to <a href="/wiki/Loiter_(aeronautics)" title="Loiter (aeronautics)">loiter</a> over a battlefield, striking once a target is located.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapon" title="Nuclear weapon">Nuclear weapons</a> use radioactive materials to create <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_fission" title="Nuclear fission">nuclear fission</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_fusion" title="Nuclear fusion">nuclear fusion</a> detonations.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Category:Primitive_weapons" title="Category:Primitive weapons">Primitive weapons</a> make little or no use of technological or industrial elements.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ranged_weapon" title="Ranged weapon">Ranged weapons</a> (unlike <a href="/wiki/Melee_weapon" title="Melee weapon">melee weapons</a>), target a distant object or person.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rocket_(weapon)" title="Rocket (weapon)">Rockets</a> are self-propelled projectiles.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Suicide_weapon" title="Suicide weapon">Suicide weapons</a> exploit the willingness of their operator not surviving the attack.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="By_target">By target</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: By target">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<dl><dd><i>– the type of target the weapon is designed to attack</i></dd></dl>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-aircraft" title="Anti-aircraft">Anti-aircraft</a> weapons target missiles and aerial vehicles in flight.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-fortification_weapon" title="Anti-fortification weapon">Anti-fortification weapons</a> are designed to target enemy installations.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anti-personnel_weapon" title="Anti-personnel weapon">Anti-personnel weapons</a> are designed to attack people, either individually or in numbers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anti-radiation_missile" title="Anti-radiation missile">Anti-radiation</a> weapons target sources of electronic radiation, particularly radar emitters.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anti-satellite_weapon" title="Anti-satellite weapon">Anti-satellite weapons</a> target orbiting satellites.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Category:Anti-ship_weapons" title="Category:Anti-ship weapons">Anti-ship weapons</a> target ships and vessels on water.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Anti-submarine_weapon" title="Anti-submarine weapon">Anti-submarine weapons</a> target submarines and other underwater targets.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anti-tank_weapon" title="Anti-tank weapon">Anti-tank weapons</a> are designed to defeat armored targets.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Area_denial_weapons" title="Area denial weapons">Area denial weapons</a> target territory, making it unsafe or unsuitable for enemy use or travel.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Hunting_weapon" title="Hunting weapon">Hunting weapons</a> are weapons used to hunt <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Game_animal" title="Game animal">game animals</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Category:Infantry_support_weapons" title="Category:Infantry support weapons">Infantry support weapons</a> are designed to attack various threats to infantry units.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Siege_engine" title="Siege engine">Siege engine</a> is designed to break or circumvent heavy fortifications in siege warfare.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Manufacture_of_weapons">Manufacture of weapons</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Manufacture of weapons">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Arms_industry" title="Arms industry">Arms industry</a></div>
<p>The arms industry is a global industry that involves the sales and manufacture of weaponry. It consists of a <a href="/wiki/Commerce" title="Commerce">commercial</a> <a href="/wiki/Industry_(economics)" title="Industry (economics)">industry</a> involved in the <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, production, and servicing of <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">military</a> material, equipment, and facilities. Many <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Industrialized_countries" title="Industrialized countries">industrialized countries</a> have a domestic arms-industry to supply their own military forces - and some also have a substantial trade in weapons for use by its citizens, for self-defence, hunting or sporting purposes.
</p><p>Contracts to supply a given country's military are awarded by governments, making arms contracts of substantial political importance. The link between politics and the arms trade can result in the development a "<a href="/wiki/Military%E2%80%93industrial_complex" title="Military–industrial complex">military–industrial complex</a>", where the armed forces, commerce, and politics become closely linked.
</p><p>According to research institute SIPRI, the volume of international transfers of major weapons in 2010–14 was 16 percent higher than in 2005–2009,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> and the arms sales of the world’s 100 largest private arms-producing and military services companies totalled  $420 billion in 2018.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Gun_laws">Gun laws</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Gun laws">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gun_laws" title="Gun laws">Gun laws</a></div>
<p>All countries have laws and policies regulating aspects such as the manufacture, sale, transfer, possession, modification and use of small arms by civilians.
</p><p>Countries which regulate access to firearms will typically restrict access to certain categories of firearms and then restrict the categories of persons who may be granted a license for access to such firearms. There may be separate licenses for hunting, sport shooting (a.k.a. target shooting), self-defense, collecting, and concealed carry, with different sets of requirements, permissions, and responsibilities.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Arms_control_laws">Arms control laws</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Arms control laws">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Arms_control" title="Arms control">Arms control</a></div>
<p>International treaties and agreements place restrictions upon the development, production, stockpiling, proliferation and usage of weapons from <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Small_arms" title="Small arms">small arms</a> and <a href="/wiki/Artillery" title="Artillery">heavy weapons</a> to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weapons_of_mass_destruction" title="Weapons of mass destruction">weapons of mass destruction</a>. Arms control is typically exercised through the use of diplomacy which seeks to impose such limitations upon consenting participants, although it may also comprise efforts by a nation or group of nations to enforce limitations upon a non-consenting country.
</p>

<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Arms_trafficking" title="Arms trafficking">Arms trafficking</a></div>
<p>Arms trafficking is the trafficking of contraband weapons and <a href="/wiki/Ammunition" title="Ammunition">ammunition</a>. What constitutes legal trade in firearms varies widely, depending on local and national laws.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lifecycle_problems">Lifecycle problems</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Lifecycle problems">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There are a number of issue around the potential ongoing risks from deployed weapons, the safe storage of weapons, and their eventual disposal when no longer effective or safe.
</p>
<ul><li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ocean_dumping" title="Ocean dumping">Ocean dumping</a> of unused weapons and bombs, including ordinary bombs, <a href="/wiki/Unexploded_ordnance" title="Unexploded ordnance">UXO</a>, landmines and chemical weapons has been common practice by many nations, and often caused hazards.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-me_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-me-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wbgh_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-wbgh-28">[28]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Unexploded_ordnance" title="Unexploded ordnance">Unexploded ordnance</a> (UXO) are bombs, land mines and naval mines and similar that did not explode when they were employed and still pose a risk for many years or decades.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Demining" title="Demining">Demining</a> or mine clearance from areas of past conflict is a difficult process, but every year, landmines kill 15,000 to 20,000 people and severely maim countless more.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Nuclear_terrorism" title="Nuclear terrorism">Nuclear terrorism</a> was a serious concern after the fall of the Soviet Union, with the prospect of "loose nukes" being available.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> While this risk may have receded,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup> similar situation may arise in the future.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_science_fiction">In science fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: In science fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Weapons_in_science_fiction" title="Weapons in science fiction">Weapons in science fiction</a></div>
<p>Strange and exotic weapons are a recurring feature or theme in <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction" title="Science fiction">science fiction</a>. In some cases, weapons first introduced in science fiction have now been made a reality. Other science fiction weapons remain purely fictional, and are often beyond the realms of known physical possibility.
</p><p>At its most prosaic, science fiction features an endless variety of <a href="/wiki/Sidearm_(weapon)" title="Sidearm (weapon)">sidearms</a>, mostly variations on real weapons such as <a href="/wiki/Gun" title="Gun">guns</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sword" title="Sword">swords</a>. Among the best-known of these are the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_weapons_in_Star_Trek#Phasers" title="List of weapons in Star Trek">phaser</a> used in the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek" title="Star Trek">Star Trek</a></i> television series, films and novels and the <a href="/wiki/Lightsaber" title="Lightsaber">lightsaber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blaster_(Star_Wars)" title="Blaster (Star Wars)">blaster</a> featured in the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars" title="Star Wars">Star Wars</a></i> movies, comics, novels and TV series.
</p><p>In addition to adding action and entertainment value, weaponry in science fiction sometimes become themes when they touch on deeper concerns, often motivated by contemporary issues. One example is science fiction that deals with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weapons_of_mass_destruction" title="Weapons of mass destruction">weapons of mass destruction</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">See also</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Weapon&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: See also">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
