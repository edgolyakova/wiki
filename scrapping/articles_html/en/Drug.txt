<h1 class="firstHeading mw-first-heading" id="firstHeading"><span class="mw-page-title-main">Drug</span></h1>
<div class="vector-body" id="bodyContent">
<div class="noprint" id="siteSub">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
<div id="contentSub"></div>
<div id="contentSub2"></div>
<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>

<div class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Substance having effect(s) on the body of an individual</div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">For other uses, see <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Drug_(disambiguation)" title="Drug (disambiguation)">Drug (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Aspirine_macro_shot.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1500" data-file-width="1500" decoding="async" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Aspirine_macro_shot.jpg/220px-Aspirine_macro_shot.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Aspirine_macro_shot.jpg/330px-Aspirine_macro_shot.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Aspirine_macro_shot.jpg/440px-Aspirine_macro_shot.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Aspirine_macro_shot.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Uncoated <a href="/wiki/Aspirin" title="Aspirin">aspirin</a> <a href="/wiki/Tablet_(pharmacy)" title="Tablet (pharmacy)">tablets</a>, consisting of about 90% acetylsalicylic acid, along with a minor amount of inert fillers and binders. Aspirin is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_drug" title="Pharmaceutical drug">pharmaceutical drug</a> often used to treat <a href="/wiki/Pain" title="Pain">pain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fever" title="Fever">fever</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Inflammation" title="Inflammation">inflammation</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>A <b>drug</b> is any <a href="/wiki/Chemical_substance" title="Chemical substance">chemical substance</a> that causes a change in an organism's <a href="/wiki/Physiology" title="Physiology">physiology</a> or <a href="/wiki/Psychology" title="Psychology">psychology</a> when consumed.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diccom_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-diccom-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-drugs.com:_Drug_Definition_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-drugs.com:_Drug_Definition-2">[2]</a></sup> Drugs are typically distinguished from <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">food</a> and substances that provide nutritional support. Consumption of drugs can be via <a href="/wiki/Insufflation_(medicine)" title="Insufflation (medicine)">inhalation</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drug_injection" title="Drug injection">injection</a>, <a href="/wiki/Smoking" title="Smoking">smoking</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ingestion" title="Ingestion">ingestion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Absorption_(skin)" title="Absorption (skin)">absorption</a> via a <a href="/wiki/Dermal_patch" title="Dermal patch">patch</a> on the skin, <a href="/wiki/Suppository" title="Suppository">suppository</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Sublingual_administration" title="Sublingual administration">dissolution under the tongue</a>.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/Pharmacology" title="Pharmacology">pharmacology</a>, a drug is a chemical substance, typically of known structure, which, when administered to a living organism, produces a biological effect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> A <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_drug" title="Pharmaceutical drug">pharmaceutical drug</a>, also called a medication or medicine, is a chemical substance used to <a href="/wiki/Pharmacotherapy" title="Pharmacotherapy">treat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cure" title="Cure">cure</a>, <a href="/wiki/Preventive_healthcare" title="Preventive healthcare">prevent</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Medical_diagnosis" title="Medical diagnosis">diagnose</a> a <a href="/wiki/Disease" title="Disease">disease</a> or to promote <a href="/wiki/Well-being" title="Well-being">well-being</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-diccom_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-diccom-1">[1]</a></sup> Traditionally drugs were obtained through extraction from <a href="/wiki/Medicinal_plants" title="Medicinal plants">medicinal plants</a>, but more recently also by <a href="/wiki/Organic_synthesis" title="Organic synthesis">organic synthesis</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> Pharmaceutical drugs may be used for a limited duration, or on a regular basis for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chronic_(medicine)" title="Chronic (medicine)">chronic disorders</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ahsci_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-ahsci-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p><p>Pharmaceutical drugs are often classified into <a href="/wiki/Drug_class" title="Drug class">drug classes</a>—groups of related drugs that have similar <a href="/wiki/Chemical_structure" title="Chemical structure">chemical structures</a>, the same <a href="/wiki/Mechanism_of_action" title="Mechanism of action">mechanism of action</a> (binding to the same <a href="/wiki/Biological_target" title="Biological target">biological target</a>), a related <a href="/wiki/Mode_of_action" title="Mode of action">mode of action</a>, and that are used to treat the same disease.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_6-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_7-0"><a href="#cite_note-auto-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Anatomical_Therapeutic_Chemical_Classification_System" title="Anatomical Therapeutic Chemical Classification System">Anatomical Therapeutic Chemical Classification System</a> (ATC), the most widely used drug classification system, assigns drugs a unique <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ATC_code" title="ATC code">ATC code</a>, which is an alphanumeric code that assigns it to specific drug classes within the ATC system. Another major classification system is the <a href="/wiki/Biopharmaceutics_Classification_System" title="Biopharmaceutics Classification System">Biopharmaceutics Classification System</a>. This classifies drugs according to their solubility and permeability or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Absorption_(pharmacokinetics)" title="Absorption (pharmacokinetics)">absorption</a> properties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Psychoactive_drug" title="Psychoactive drug">Psychoactive drugs</a> are chemical substances that affect the function of the <a href="/wiki/Central_nervous_system" title="Central nervous system">central nervous system</a>, altering <a href="/wiki/Perception" title="Perception">perception</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mood_(psychology)" title="Mood (psychology)">mood</a> or <a href="/wiki/Consciousness" title="Consciousness">consciousness</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bushbook_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-bushbook-9">[9]</a></sup> These drugs are divided into different groups like: <a href="/wiki/Stimulant" title="Stimulant">stimulants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Depressant" title="Depressant">depressants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antidepressant" title="Antidepressant">antidepressants</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anxiolytic" title="Anxiolytic">anxiolytics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antipsychotic" title="Antipsychotic">antipsychotics</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Hallucinogen" title="Hallucinogen">hallucinogens</a>. These psychoactive drugs have been proven useful in treating wide range of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medical_conditions" title="Medical conditions">medical conditions</a> including <a href="/wiki/Mental_disorder" title="Mental disorder">mental disorders</a> around the world. The most widely used drugs in the world include <a href="/wiki/Caffeine" title="Caffeine">caffeine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicotine" title="Nicotine">nicotine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alcohol_(drug)" title="Alcohol (drug)">alcohol</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> which are also considered <a href="/wiki/Recreational_drug_use" title="Recreational drug use">recreational drugs</a>, since they are used for pleasure rather than medicinal purposes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> All drugs can have potential <a href="/wiki/Side_effect" title="Side effect">side effects</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MHRA_Side_Effects_of_Medicines_12-0"><a href="#cite_note-MHRA_Side_Effects_of_Medicines-12">[12]</a></sup> Abuse of several psychoactive drugs can cause <a href="/wiki/Addiction" title="Addiction">addiction</a> and/or <a href="/wiki/Substance_dependence" title="Substance dependence">physical dependence</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Excessive use of stimulants can promote <a href="/wiki/Stimulant_psychosis" title="Stimulant psychosis">stimulant psychosis</a>. Many recreational drugs are <a href="/wiki/Illegal_drug_trade" title="Illegal drug trade">illicit</a> and international treaties such as the <a href="/wiki/Single_Convention_on_Narcotic_Drugs" title="Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs">Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs</a> exist for the purpose of their <a href="/wiki/Prohibition_of_drugs" title="Prohibition of drugs">prohibition</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">Etymology</span></h2>
<p>In English, the noun "drug" is thought to originate from Old French "drogue", possibly deriving from "droge (vate)" from Middle Dutch meaning "dry (barrels)", referring to medicinal plants preserved as dry matter in barrels.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-etymonline-drug_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-etymonline-drug-14">[14]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Medication">Medication</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nexium_(esomeprazole_magnesium)_pills.JPG"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="958" data-file-width="932" decoding="async" height="226" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Nexium_%28esomeprazole_magnesium%29_pills.JPG/220px-Nexium_%28esomeprazole_magnesium%29_pills.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Nexium_%28esomeprazole_magnesium%29_pills.JPG/330px-Nexium_%28esomeprazole_magnesium%29_pills.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Nexium_%28esomeprazole_magnesium%29_pills.JPG/440px-Nexium_%28esomeprazole_magnesium%29_pills.JPG 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Nexium_(esomeprazole_magnesium)_pills.JPG" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nexium" title="Nexium">Nexium</a> (Esomeprazole) is a <a href="/wiki/Proton-pump_inhibitor" title="Proton-pump inhibitor">proton-pump inhibitor</a>. It is used to reduce the production of stomach acid.</div></div></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_drug" title="Pharmaceutical drug">Pharmaceutical drug</a>, <a href="/wiki/Drug_class" title="Drug class">Drug class</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Medication" title="Medication">Medication</a></div>
<p>A <i>medication</i> or <i>medicine</i> is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_drug" title="Pharmaceutical drug">drug</a> taken to cure or ameliorate any symptoms of an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Illness" title="Illness">illness</a> or medical condition. The use may also be as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Preventive_medicine" title="Preventive medicine">preventive medicine</a> that has future benefits but does not treat any existing or pre-existing diseases or symptoms. Dispensing of medication is often regulated by <a href="/wiki/Government" title="Government">governments</a> into three categories—<i><a href="/wiki/Over-the-counter_drug" title="Over-the-counter drug">over-the-counter</a></i> medications, which are available in <a href="/wiki/Pharmacy" title="Pharmacy">pharmacies</a> and supermarkets without special restrictions; <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Behind-the-counter" title="Behind-the-counter">behind-the-counter</a></i> medicines, which are dispensed by a <a href="/wiki/Pharmacist" title="Pharmacist">pharmacist</a> without needing a doctor's prescription, and <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prescription_medication" title="Prescription medication">prescription only medicines</a></i>, which must be prescribed by a licensed <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medical_professional" title="Medical professional">medical professional</a>, usually a <a href="/wiki/Physician" title="Physician">physician</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPC_16-0"><a href="#cite_note-HPC-16">[16]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the United Kingdom, behind-the-counter medicines are called <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmacy_medicine" title="Pharmacy medicine">pharmacy medicines</a> which can only be sold in registered pharmacies, by or under the supervision of a pharmacist. These medications are designated by the letter P on the label.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> The range of medicines available without a prescription varies from country to country. Medications are typically produced by <a href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_industry" title="Pharmaceutical industry">pharmaceutical companies</a> and are often <a href="/wiki/Patent" title="Patent">patented</a> to give the developer exclusive rights to produce them. Those that are not patented (or with expired patents) are called <a href="/wiki/Generic_drug" title="Generic drug">generic drugs</a> since they can be produced by other companies without restrictions or licenses from the patent holder.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>Pharmaceutical drugs are usually categorised into <a href="/wiki/Drug_class" title="Drug class">drug classes</a>. A group of drugs will share a similar <a href="/wiki/Chemical_structure" title="Chemical structure">chemical structure</a>, or have the same <a href="/wiki/Mechanism_of_action" title="Mechanism of action">mechanism of action</a>, the same related <a href="/wiki/Mode_of_action" title="Mode of action">mode of action</a> or target the same illness or related illnesses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_6-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-6">[6]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-auto_7-1"><a href="#cite_note-auto-7">[7]</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Anatomical_Therapeutic_Chemical_Classification_System" title="Anatomical Therapeutic Chemical Classification System">Anatomical Therapeutic Chemical Classification System</a> (ATC), the most widely used drug classification system, assigns drugs a unique <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ATC_code" title="ATC code">ATC code</a>, which is an alphanumeric code that assigns it to specific drug classes within the ATC system. Another major classification system is the <a href="/wiki/Biopharmaceutics_Classification_System" title="Biopharmaceutics Classification System">Biopharmaceutics Classification System</a>. This groups drugs according to their solubility and permeability or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Absorption_(pharmacokinetics)" title="Absorption (pharmacokinetics)">absorption</a> properties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceB_8-1"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceB-8">[8]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Spiritual_and_religious_use">Spiritual and religious use</span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Entheogen" title="Entheogen">Entheogen</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychonaut" title="Psychonaut">Psychonaut</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chaman_amazonie_5_06.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3504" data-file-width="2336" decoding="async" height="330" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Chaman_amazonie_5_06.jpg/220px-Chaman_amazonie_5_06.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Chaman_amazonie_5_06.jpg/330px-Chaman_amazonie_5_06.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Chaman_amazonie_5_06.jpg/440px-Chaman_amazonie_5_06.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chaman_amazonie_5_06.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An <a href="/wiki/Shamanism#Amazonia" title="Shamanism">Amazonian shaman</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Flowering_San_Pedro_cactus.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2048" data-file-width="1536" decoding="async" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Flowering_San_Pedro_cactus.jpg/220px-Flowering_San_Pedro_cactus.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Flowering_San_Pedro_cactus.jpg/330px-Flowering_San_Pedro_cactus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Flowering_San_Pedro_cactus.jpg/440px-Flowering_San_Pedro_cactus.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Flowering_San_Pedro_cactus.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Echinopsis_pachanoi" title="Echinopsis pachanoi">San Pedro</a>, a psychoactive cactus.</div></div></div>
<p>Some religions, particularly <a href="/wiki/Ethnic_religion" title="Ethnic religion">ethnic religions</a>, are based completely on the use of certain drugs, known as <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Entheogens" title="Entheogens">entheogens</a>, which are mostly <a href="/wiki/Hallucinogen" title="Hallucinogen">hallucinogens</a>,—<a href="/wiki/Psychedelic_drug" title="Psychedelic drug">psychedelics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dissociative" title="Dissociative">dissociatives</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Deliriant" title="Deliriant">deliriants</a>. Some drugs used as entheogens include <a href="/wiki/Kava" title="Kava">kava</a> which can act as a <a href="/wiki/Stimulant" title="Stimulant">stimulant</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Sedative" title="Sedative">sedative</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euphoriant" title="Euphoriant">euphoriant</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Anesthetic" title="Anesthetic">anesthetic</a>. The roots of the kava plant are used to produce a drink which is consumed throughout the cultures of the Pacific Ocean.
</p><p>Some <a href="/wiki/Shamanism" title="Shamanism">shamans</a> from different cultures use entheogens, defined as "generating the divine within"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup> to achieve <a href="/wiki/Religious_ecstasy" title="Religious ecstasy">religious ecstasy</a>. Amazonian shamans use <a href="/wiki/Ayahuasca" title="Ayahuasca">ayahuasca</a> (yagé) a hallucinogenic brew for this purpose. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mazatec_shamans" title="Mazatec shamans">Mazatec shamans</a> have a long and continuous tradition of religious use of <i><a href="/wiki/Salvia_divinorum" title="Salvia divinorum">Salvia divinorum</a></i> a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Psychoactive" title="Psychoactive">psychoactive</a> plant. Its use is to facilitate <a href="/wiki/Altered_state_of_consciousness" title="Altered state of consciousness">visionary states of consciousness</a> during spiritual healing sessions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Valdés1983p287_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Valdés1983p287-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Silene_undulata" title="Silene undulata">Silene undulata</a> is regarded by the <a href="/wiki/Xhosa_people" title="Xhosa people">Xhosa people</a> as a sacred plant and used as an entheogen. Its roots are traditionally used to induce vivid (and according to the Xhosa, prophetic) <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lucid_dreaming" title="Lucid dreaming">lucid dreams</a> during the initiation process of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shamans" title="Shamans">shamans</a>, classifying it a naturally occurring <a href="/wiki/Oneirogen" title="Oneirogen">oneirogen</a> similar to the more well-known dream herb <i><a href="/wiki/Calea_ternifolia" title="Calea ternifolia">Calea ternifolia</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Peyote" title="Peyote">Peyote</a>, a small spineless <a href="/wiki/Cactus" title="Cactus">cactus</a>, has been a major source of psychedelic <a href="/wiki/Mescaline" title="Mescaline">mescaline</a> and has probably been used by <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">Native Americans</a> for at least five thousand years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prehistoric_22-0"><a href="#cite_note-prehistoric-22">[22]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Most mescaline is now obtained from a few species of columnar cacti in particular from <a href="/wiki/Echinopsis_pachanoi" title="Echinopsis pachanoi">San Pedro</a> and not from the vulnerable peyote.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Entheogenic_use_of_cannabis" title="Entheogenic use of cannabis">entheogenic use of cannabis</a> has also been widely practised<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup> for centuries.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rastafari" title="Rastafari">Rastafari</a> use <a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">marijuana</a> (ganja) as a <a href="/wiki/Sacrament" title="Sacrament">sacrament</a> in their <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Abrahamic_religion" title="Abrahamic religion">religious ceremonies</a>.
</p><p>Psychedelic mushrooms (<a href="/wiki/Psilocybin_mushroom" title="Psilocybin mushroom">psilocybin mushrooms</a>), commonly called <i>magic mushrooms</i> or <i>shrooms</i> have also long been used as entheogens.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Smart_drugs_and_designer_drugs">Smart drugs and designer drugs</span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Nootropic" title="Nootropic">Nootropic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Designer_drug" title="Designer drug">Designer drug</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Psychoactive_drug" title="Psychoactive drug">Psychoactive drug</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Nootropic" title="Nootropic">Nootropics</a>, also commonly referred to as "smart drugs", are drugs that are claimed to improve human <a href="/wiki/Cognition" title="Cognition">cognitive abilities</a>. Nootropics are used to improve memory, concentration, thought, mood, and learning. An increasingly used nootropic among students, also known as a <i>study drug</i>,  is <a href="/wiki/Methylphenidate" title="Methylphenidate">methylphenidate</a> branded commonly as <i>Ritalin</i> and used for the treatment of <a href="/wiki/Attention_deficit_hyperactivity_disorder" title="Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder">attention deficit hyperactivity disorder</a> (ADHD) and <a href="/wiki/Narcolepsy" title="Narcolepsy">narcolepsy</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Abelman_27-0"><a href="#cite_note-Abelman-27">[27]</a></sup> At high doses methylphenidate <a href="/wiki/Methylphenidate#Biochemocal_mechanisms" title="Methylphenidate">can become highly addictive</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Smith_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Smith-28">[28]</a></sup> Serious addiction can lead to <a href="/wiki/Psychosis" title="Psychosis">psychosis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anxiety" title="Anxiety">anxiety</a> and heart problems, and the use of this drug is related to a rise in suicides, and overdoses. Evidence for use outside of student settings is limited but suggests that it is commonplace.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Abelman_27-1"><a href="#cite_note-Abelman-27">[27]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Smith_28-1"><a href="#cite_note-Smith-28">[28]</a></sup> Intravenous use of methylphenidate can lead to <a href="/wiki/Pneumatosis#Pulmonary_emphysema" title="Pneumatosis">emphysematous</a> damage to the lungs, known as <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ritalin_lung" title="Ritalin lung">Ritalin lung</a></i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup>
</p><p>Other drugs known as <a href="/wiki/Designer_drug" title="Designer drug">designer drugs</a> are produced. An early example of what today would be labelled a 'designer drug' was <a href="/wiki/Lysergic_acid_diethylamide" title="Lysergic acid diethylamide">LSD</a>, which was synthesised from <a href="/wiki/Ergot" title="Ergot">ergot</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> Other examples include analogs of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Performance-enhancing_drugs" title="Performance-enhancing drugs">performance-enhancing drugs</a> such as designer <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Steroids" title="Steroids">steroids</a> taken to improve physical capabilities and these are sometimes used (legally or not) for this purpose, often by professional athletes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid22191595_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-pmid22191595-31">[31]</a></sup> Other designer drugs mimic the effects of psychoactive drugs. Since the late 1990s there has been the identification of many of these synthesised drugs. In Japan and the United Kingdom this has spurred the addition of many designer drugs into a newer class of controlled substances known as a <a href="/wiki/Temporary_class_drug" title="Temporary class drug">temporary class drug</a>.
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synthetic_cannabinoid" title="Synthetic cannabinoid">Synthetic cannabinoids</a> have been produced for a longer period of time and are used in the designer drug <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synthetic_cannabis" title="Synthetic cannabis">synthetic cannabis</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recreational_drug_use">Recreational drug use</span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Recreational_drug_use" title="Recreational drug use">Recreational drug use</a></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Prohibition_of_drugs" title="Prohibition of drugs">Prohibition of drugs</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Macro_cannabis_bud.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1592" data-file-width="1759" decoding="async" height="199" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Macro_cannabis_bud.jpg/220px-Macro_cannabis_bud.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Macro_cannabis_bud.jpg/330px-Macro_cannabis_bud.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Macro_cannabis_bud.jpg/440px-Macro_cannabis_bud.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Macro_cannabis_bud.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">Cannabis</a> is a commonly used <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Recreational_drug" title="Recreational drug">recreational drug</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Recreational_drug_use" title="Recreational drug use">Recreational drug use</a> is the use of a drug (legal, controlled, or illegal) with the primary intention of <a href="/wiki/Altered_state_of_consciousness" title="Altered state of consciousness">altering the state of consciousness</a> through alteration of the central nervous system in order to create positive emotions and feelings. The hallucinogen <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LSD" title="LSD">LSD</a> is a psychoactive drug commonly used as a recreational drug.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ketamine" title="Ketamine">Ketamine</a> is a drug used for <a href="/wiki/Anesthesia" title="Anesthesia">anesthesia</a>, and is also used as a recreational drug, both in powder and liquid form, for its hallucinogenic and <a href="/wiki/Dissociation_(psychology)" title="Dissociation (psychology)">dissociative effects</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Morgan_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-Morgan-34">[34]</a></sup>
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drug_laws" title="Drug laws">Some national laws</a> prohibit the use of different recreational drugs; and medicinal drugs that have the potential for recreational use are often heavily regulated. However, there are many recreational drugs that are <a href="/wiki/Drug_liberalization#Policies_by_country" title="Drug liberalization">legal in many jurisdictions</a> and widely culturally accepted.
<a href="/wiki/Cannabis_(drug)" title="Cannabis (drug)">Cannabis</a> is the most commonly consumed controlled recreational drug in the world (as of 2012).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WDR_2012_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-WDR_2012-35">[35]</a></sup> Its use in many countries is illegal but is <a href="/wiki/Legality_of_cannabis" title="Legality of cannabis">legally used</a> in several countries usually with the proviso that it can only be used for personal use. It can be used in the <i>leaf</i> form of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marijuana" title="Marijuana">marijuana</a> <i>(grass)</i>, or in the resin form of <a href="/wiki/Hashish" title="Hashish">hashish</a>. Marijuana is a more mild form of cannabis than hashish.
</p><p>There may be an age restriction on the consumption and purchase of legal recreational drugs. Some recreational drugs that are legal and accepted in many places include <a href="/wiki/Alcoholic_beverage" title="Alcoholic beverage">alcohol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">tobacco</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Betel_nut" title="Betel nut">betel nut</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Caffeine" title="Caffeine">caffeine</a> products, and in some areas of the world the legal use of drugs such as <a href="/wiki/Khat" title="Khat">khat</a> is common.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kciy_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-Kciy-36">[36]</a></sup>
</p><p>There are a number of legal intoxicants commonly called <i>legal highs</i> that are used recreationally. The most widely used of these is alcohol.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration_of_drugs">Administration of drugs</span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Route_of_administration" title="Route of administration">Route of administration</a></div>
<p>All drugs, can be <a href="/wiki/Drug_delivery" title="Drug delivery">administered</a> via a number of <a href="/wiki/Route_of_administration" title="Route of administration">routes</a>, and many can be administered by more than one.
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r998391716">.mw-parser-output .div-col{margin-top:0.3em;column-width:30em}.mw-parser-output .div-col-small{font-size:90%}.mw-parser-output .div-col-rules{column-rule:1px solid #aaa}.mw-parser-output .div-col dl,.mw-parser-output .div-col ol,.mw-parser-output .div-col ul{margin-top:0}.mw-parser-output .div-col li,.mw-parser-output .div-col dd{page-break-inside:avoid;break-inside:avoid-column}</style><div class="div-col" style="column-width: 30em;">
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Bolus_(medicine)" title="Bolus (medicine)">Bolus</a> is the administration of a medication, drug or other compound that is given to raise its concentration in blood to an effective level. The administration can be given intravenously, by parenteral,by indovenous, by intramuscular, intrathecal or subcutaneous injection.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Inhalation" title="Inhalation">Inhaled</a>, (breathed into the lungs), as an <a href="/wiki/Aerosol" title="Aerosol">aerosol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inhaler" title="Inhaler">inhaler</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vape" title="Vape">vape</a> or dry powder (this includes smoking or vaping a substance).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Route_of_administration#Injection" title="Route of administration">Injection</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Solution_(chemistry)" title="Solution (chemistry)">solution</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(chemistry)" title="Suspension (chemistry)">suspension</a> or <a href="/wiki/Emulsion" title="Emulsion">emulsion</a> either: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intramuscular" title="Intramuscular">intramuscular</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intravenous" title="Intravenous">intravenous</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intraperitoneal" title="Intraperitoneal">intraperitoneal</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intraosseous" title="Intraosseous">intraosseous</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Insufflation_(medicine)" title="Insufflation (medicine)">Insufflation</a>, as a <a href="/wiki/Nasal_spray" title="Nasal spray">nasal spray</a> or snorting into the nose.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Oral_administration" title="Oral administration">Orally</a>, as a liquid or solid, that is absorbed through the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intestine" title="Intestine">intestines</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Rectum" title="Rectum">Rectally</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Suppository" title="Suppository">suppository</a>, that is absorbed by the rectum or colon.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sublingual_administration" title="Sublingual administration">Sublingually</a>, diffusing into the blood through tissues under the tongue.</li>
<li><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Topical" title="Topical">Topically</a>, usually as a <a href="/wiki/Cream" title="Cream">cream</a> or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ointment" title="Ointment">ointment</a>. A drug administered in this manner may be given to act locally or systemically.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Vagina" title="Vagina">Vaginally</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Pessary#Pharmaceutical_pessaries" title="Pessary">pessary</a>, primarily to treat vaginal infections.</li></ul></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Control_of_drugs">Control of drugs</span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Prohibition_of_drugs" title="Prohibition of drugs">Prohibition of drugs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drug_policy" title="Drug policy">Drug policy</a></div>
<p>There are numerous governmental offices in many countries that deal with the control and oversee of drug manufacture and use, and the implementation of various drug laws. The <a href="/wiki/Single_Convention_on_Narcotic_Drugs" title="Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs">Single Convention on Narcotic Drugs</a> is an international <a href="/wiki/Treaty" title="Treaty">treaty</a> brought about in 1961 to prohibit the use of narcotics save for those used in <a href="/wiki/Medical_research" title="Medical research">medical research</a> and treatment. In 1971, a second treaty the <a href="/wiki/Convention_on_Psychotropic_Substances" title="Convention on Psychotropic Substances">Convention on Psychotropic Substances</a> had to be introduced to deal with newer recreational psychoactive and psychedelic drugs.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Legal_status_of_Salvia_divinorum" title="Legal status of Salvia divinorum">legal status of Salvia divinorum</a> varies in many countries and even in <a href="/wiki/Legal_status_of_Salvia_divinorum_in_the_United_States" title="Legal status of Salvia divinorum in the United States">states within the United States</a>. Where it is legislated against the degree of prohibition also varies.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Food_and_Drug_Administration" title="Food and Drug Administration">Food and Drug Administration</a> (FDA) in the United States is a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_United_States_federal_agencies" title="List of United States federal agencies">federal agency</a> responsible for protecting and promoting <a href="/wiki/Public_health" title="Public health">public health</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Regulation" title="Regulation">regulation</a> and supervision of <a href="/wiki/Food_safety" title="Food safety">food safety</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tobacco_products" title="Tobacco products">tobacco products</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dietary_supplement" title="Dietary supplement">dietary supplements</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prescription_drug" title="Prescription drug">prescription</a> and <a href="/wiki/Over-the-counter_drug" title="Over-the-counter drug">over-the-counter</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pharmaceutical_drug" title="Pharmaceutical drug">medications</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vaccine" title="Vaccine">vaccines</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biopharmaceutical" title="Biopharmaceutical">biopharmaceuticals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blood_transfusion" title="Blood transfusion">blood transfusions</a>, <a href="/wiki/Medical_device" title="Medical device">medical devices</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_radiation" title="Electromagnetic radiation">electromagnetic radiation</a> emitting devices, <a href="/wiki/Cosmetics" title="Cosmetics">cosmetics</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animal_Feed" title="Animal Feed">animal foods</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Veterinary_medicine" title="Veterinary medicine">veterinary drugs</a>.
</p><p>In India, the <a href="/wiki/Narcotics_Control_Bureau" title="Narcotics Control Bureau">Narcotics Control Bureau</a> (abbr. <b>NCB</b>), an <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_in_India" title="Law enforcement in India">Indian federal law enforcement</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Indian_Intelligence_agencies" title="List of Indian Intelligence agencies">intelligence</a> agency under the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Home_Affairs_(India)" title="Ministry of Home Affairs (India)">Ministry of Home Affairs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Government_of_India" title="Government of India">Government of India</a> is tasked with combating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drug_trafficking" title="Drug trafficking">drug trafficking</a> and assisting international use of illegal substances under the provisions of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Narcotic_Drugs_and_Psychotropic_Substances_Act_(India)" title="Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act (India)">Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup>
</p>
