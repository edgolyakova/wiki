<p><b>History of North America</b> encompasses the past developments of people populating the continent of <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a>. While it was widely believed that continent first became a human habitat when people migrated across the <a href="/wiki/Bering_Sea" title="Bering Sea">Bering Sea</a> 40,000 to 17,000 years ago,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-national_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-national-1">[1]</a></sup> recent discoveries may have pushed those estimates back at least another 90,000 years.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> People <a href="/wiki/Settlement_of_the_Americas" title="Settlement of the Americas">settled</a> throughout the continent, from the <a href="/wiki/Inuit" title="Inuit">Inuit</a> of the far north to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mayans" title="Mayans">Mayans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aztecs" title="Aztecs">Aztecs</a> of the south. These complex communities each developed their own unique ways of life and cultures.
</p><p>Records of European travel to North America begin with the <a href="/wiki/Norse_colonization_of_North_America" title="Norse colonization of North America">Norse colonization</a> in the tenth century AD. In 985, they founded a settlement on <a href="/wiki/History_of_Greenland" title="History of Greenland">Greenland</a> (an often-overlooked part of North America) that persisted until the early 1400s. They also explored the east coast of Canada, but their settlements there were much smaller and shorter-lived.  With the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Age_of_Exploration" title="Age of Exploration">Age of Exploration</a> and the voyages of <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> (starting 1492), <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Colonization of the Americas">Europeans began to arrive</a> in the Americas in large numbers and to develop colonial ambitions for both North and South America.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> After Columbus, influxes of Europeans soon followed and overwhelmed the native population. North America became a staging ground for ongoing European rivalries. The continent was divided by three prominent European powers: <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_colonial_empire" title="French colonial empire">France</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spain</a>. The influences of colonization by these states on North American cultures are still apparent today.
</p><p>Conflict over resources on North America ensued in various wars between these powers, but, gradually, the new European colonies developed desires for independence. Revolutions, such as the <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mexican_War_of_Independence" title="Mexican War of Independence">Mexican War of Independence</a>, created new, independent states that came to dominate North America. The <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Confederation" title="Canadian Confederation">Canadian Confederation</a> formed in 1867, creating the modern political landscape of North America.
</p><p>From the 19th to 21st centuries, North American states have developed increasingly deeper connections with each other. Although some conflicts have occurred, the continent has for the most part enjoyed peace and general cooperation between its states, as well as open commerce and trade between them. Modern developments include the opening of free trade agreements, extensive immigration from Mexico and Latin America, and drug trafficking concerns in these regions.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_beginning_of_North_America">The beginning of North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The beginning of North America">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Settlement_of_the_Americas" title="Settlement of the Americas">Settlement of the Americas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paleo-Indians" title="Paleo-Indians">Paleo-Indians</a></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Genetic_history_of_indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Genetic history of indigenous peoples of the Americas">Genetic history of indigenous peoples of the Americas</a></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Spreading_homo_sapiens_la.svg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="400" data-file-width="851" decoding="async" height="141" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Spreading_homo_sapiens_la.svg/300px-Spreading_homo_sapiens_la.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Spreading_homo_sapiens_la.svg/450px-Spreading_homo_sapiens_la.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Spreading_homo_sapiens_la.svg/600px-Spreading_homo_sapiens_la.svg.png 2x" width="300"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Spreading_homo_sapiens_la.svg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map of <a href="/wiki/Early_human_migrations" title="Early human migrations">early human migrations</a> based on the <a href="/wiki/Recent_African_origin_of_modern_humans" title="Recent African origin of modern humans">Out of Africa theory</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>The specifics of <a href="/wiki/Paleo-Indians" title="Paleo-Indians">Paleo-Indians</a>' migration to and throughout the Americas, including the exact dates and routes traveled, are subject to ongoing research and discussion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-national_1-1"><a href="#cite_note-national-1">[1]</a></sup> For years, the traditional theory has been that these early migrants moved into the <a href="/wiki/Beringia" title="Beringia">Beringia land bridge</a> between eastern Siberia and present-day Alaska around 40,000–17,000 years ago, when sea levels were significantly lowered due to the <a href="/wiki/Quaternary_glaciation" title="Quaternary glaciation">Quaternary glaciation</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-national_1-2"><a href="#cite_note-national-1">[1]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Smithsonian_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-Smithsonian-4">[4]</a></sup> These people are believed to have followed herds of now-extinct <a href="/wiki/Pleistocene" title="Pleistocene">pleistocene</a> <a href="/wiki/Megafauna" title="Megafauna">megafauna</a> along ice-free corridors that stretched between the <a href="/wiki/Laurentide_Ice_Sheet" title="Laurentide Ice Sheet">Laurentide</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cordilleran_Ice_Sheet" title="Cordilleran Ice Sheet">Cordilleran</a> ice sheets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> Another route proposed is that, either on foot or using <a href="/wiki/Boat#History" title="Boat">primitive boats</a>, they migrated down the Pacific coast to <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">South America</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup> Evidence of the latter would since have been covered by a <a href="/wiki/Sea_level_rise" title="Sea level rise">sea level rise</a> of hundreds of meters following the last ice age.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup>
</p><p>Archaeologists contend that Paleo-Indian migration out of Beringia (<a href="/wiki/Geography_of_Alaska" title="Geography of Alaska">eastern Alaska</a>), ranges from 40,000 to around 16,500 years ago.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-First_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-First-8">[8]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kind_11-0"><a href="#cite_note-kind-11">[11]</a></sup> This time range is a hot source of debate and promises to continue as such for years to come. The few agreements achieved to date are the origin from Central Asia, with widespread habitation of the Americas during the end of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Last_glacial_period" title="Last glacial period">last glacial period</a>, or more specifically what is known as the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Late_Glacial_Maximum#North_America" title="Late Glacial Maximum">late glacial maximum</a>, around 16,000–13,000 years before present.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kind_11-1"><a href="#cite_note-kind-11">[11]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> However, older <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pre-Columbian_trans-oceanic_contact" title="Pre-Columbian trans-oceanic contact">alternative theories</a> exist, including migration from Europe.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_13-0"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-13">[13]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:131px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Folsom_point.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="230" data-file-width="129" decoding="async" height="230" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f8/Folsom_point.png" width="129"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Folsom_point.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Folsom_point" title="Folsom point">Folsom projectile point</a>.</div></div></div>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stone_tools" title="Stone tools">Stone tools</a>, particularly <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Projectile_points" title="Projectile points">projectile points</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scraper_(archaeology)" title="Scraper (archaeology)">scrapers</a>, are the primary evidence of early human activity in the Americas. Crafted <a href="/wiki/Lithic_flake" title="Lithic flake">lithic flaked</a> tools are used by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archaeologists" title="Archaeologists">archaeologists</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anthropologists" title="Anthropologists">anthropologists</a> to classify cultural periods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Phillips_14-0"><a href="#cite_note-Phillips-14">[14]</a></sup> Scientific evidence links <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">indigenous Americans</a> to Asian peoples, specifically eastern <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_Siberia" title="Indigenous peoples of Siberia">Siberian populations</a>. Indigenous peoples of the Americas have been linked to North Asian populations by <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_languages_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous languages of the Americas">linguistic dialects</a>, the distribution of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blood_types" title="Blood types">blood types</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Genetic_code" title="Genetic code">genetic composition</a> as reflected by <a href="/wiki/Molecule" title="Molecule">molecular</a> data, such as <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Genebase_15-0"><a href="#cite_note-Genebase-15">[15]</a></sup> 8,000–7,000 BCE (10,000–9,000 years ago) the climate stabilized, leading to a rise in population and <a href="/wiki/Lithic_technology" title="Lithic technology">lithic technology</a> advances, resulting in a more <a href="/wiki/Sedentary_lifestyle" title="Sedentary lifestyle">sedentary lifestyle</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-Columbian_era">Pre-Columbian era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-Columbian era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Pre-Columbian_era#North_America" title="Pre-Columbian era">Pre-Columbian era § North America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_North_American_prehistory" title="Timeline of North American prehistory">Timeline of North American prehistory</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous peoples of the Americas">Indigenous peoples of the Americas</a></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aboriginal_peoples_in_Canada" title="Aboriginal peoples in Canada">Aboriginal peoples in Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native Americans in the United States</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mesoamerica" title="Mesoamerica">Mesoamerica</a></div>
<p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pre-Columbian" title="Pre-Columbian">Before contact with Europeans</a>, the indigenous peoples of North America were divided into many different <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polities" title="Polities">polities</a>, from small <a href="/wiki/Band_society" title="Band society">bands</a> of a few families to large empires. They lived in numerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Classification_of_indigenous_peoples_of_the_Americas" title="Classification of indigenous peoples of the Americas">culture areas</a>, which roughly correspond to <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_North_America" title="Geography of North America">geographic and biological zones</a>. Societies adapted their subsistence strategies to their homelands, and some societies were <a href="/wiki/Hunter-gatherer" title="Hunter-gatherer">hunter-gatherers</a>, some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Horticulturists" title="Horticulturists">horticulturists</a>, some <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agriculturalist" title="Agriculturalist">agriculturalists</a>, and many a mix of these. Native groups can also be classified by their <a href="/wiki/Indigenous_languages_of_the_Americas" title="Indigenous languages of the Americas">language family</a> (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Athabaskan_languages" title="Athabaskan languages">Athapascan</a> or <a href="/wiki/Uto-Aztecan_languages" title="Uto-Aztecan languages">Uto-Aztecan</a>). It is important to note that people with similar languages did not always share the same <a href="/wiki/Material_culture" title="Material culture">material culture</a>, nor were they always <a href="/wiki/Alliance" title="Alliance">allies</a>.
</p><p>The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Archaic_period_in_the_Americas" title="Archaic period in the Americas">Archaic period in the Americas</a> saw a changing environment featuring a warmer more <a href="/wiki/Arid" title="Arid">arid</a> climate and the disappearance of the last megafauna.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> The majority of population groups at this time were still highly mobile hunter-gatherers; but now individual groups started to focus on resources available to them locally, thus with the passage of time there is a pattern of increasing regional generalization, for example the <a href="/wiki/Archaic_Southwest" title="Archaic Southwest">Southwest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paleo-Arctic_tradition" title="Paleo-Arctic tradition">Arctic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Poverty_Point_culture" title="Poverty Point culture">Poverty Point culture</a>, <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Plains_Arctic&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Plains Arctic (page does not exist)">Plains Arctic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dalton_tradition" title="Dalton tradition">Dalton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Plano_cultures" title="Plano cultures">Plano</a> traditions. This kind of regional adaptation became the norm, with reliance less on hunting and gathering among many cultures, with a more mixed economy of small game, fish, seasonally <a href="/wiki/Vegetable" title="Vegetable">wild vegetables</a> and harvested plant foods.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Fiedel_17-0"><a href="#cite_note-Fiedel-17">[17]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Many groups continued as big game hunters, but their hunting traditions became more varied, and meat procurement methods more sophisticated.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pielou_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-Pielou-19">[19]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gill_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-gill-20">[20]</a></sup> The placement of artifacts and materials within an Archaic burial site indicated social differentiation based upon status in some groups.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-icaage_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-icaage-21">[21]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Palenque_Ruins.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Palenque_Ruins.jpg/220px-Palenque_Ruins.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Palenque_Ruins.jpg/330px-Palenque_Ruins.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Palenque_Ruins.jpg/440px-Palenque_Ruins.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Palenque_Ruins.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Mayan</a> ruins of <a href="/wiki/Palenque" title="Palenque">Palenque</a>, Mexico</div></div></div>
<p>Agriculture was invented independently in two regions of North America: the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Woodlands" title="Eastern Woodlands">Eastern Woodlands</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Mesoamerica" title="Mesoamerica">Mesoamerica</a>. The more southern cultural groups of North America were responsible for the <a href="/wiki/Domestication" title="Domestication">domestication</a> of many common crops now used around the world, such as tomatoes and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squash_(plant)" title="Squash (plant)">squash</a>. Perhaps most importantly they domesticated one of the world's major staples, maize (corn). During the <a href="/wiki/Plains_Village_period" title="Plains Village period">Plains Village period</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agriculture_on_the_prehistoric_Great_Plains" title="Agriculture on the prehistoric Great Plains">agriculture</a> and bison-hunting were important to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Plains_tribes" title="Great Plains tribes">Great Plains tribes</a>.
</p><p>As a result of the development of agriculture in the south, many important cultural advances were made there. For example, the <a href="/wiki/Maya_civilization" title="Maya civilization">Maya civilization</a> developed a <a href="/wiki/Maya_script" title="Maya script">writing system</a>, built <a href="/wiki/Mesoamerican_pyramids" title="Mesoamerican pyramids">huge pyramids</a>, had a <a href="/wiki/Maya_calendar" title="Maya calendar">complex calendar</a>, and developed the concept of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/0_(number)" title="0 (number)">zero</a> 500 years before anyone in the <a href="/wiki/Old_World" title="Old World">Old World</a>. The Mayan culture was still present when the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Conquistadors" title="Conquistadors">Spanish</a> arrived in Central America, but political dominance in the area had shifted to the <a href="/wiki/Aztec_Empire" title="Aztec Empire">Aztec Empire</a> further north.
</p><p>In the Southwest of North America, <a href="/wiki/Hohokam" title="Hohokam">Hohokam</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ancestral_Pueblo" title="Ancestral Pueblo">Ancestral Pueblo</a> societies had been engaged in agricultural production with ditch irrigation and a sedentary village life for at least two millennia before the Spanish arrived in the 1540s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup> Upon the arrival of the Europeans in the "New World", native peoples found their culture changed drastically. As such, their affiliation with political and cultural groups changed as well, several linguistic groups went <a href="/wiki/Language_death" title="Language death">extinct</a>, and others changed quite quickly. The name and cultures that Europeans recorded for the natives were not necessarily the same as the ones they had used a few generations before, or the ones in use today.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Arrival_of_Europeans">Arrival of Europeans</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Arrival of Europeans">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_contact">Early contact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Early contact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Historisch_Nordamerika_(cropped).jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1682" data-file-width="2249" decoding="async" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Historisch_Nordamerika_%28cropped%29.jpg/200px-Historisch_Nordamerika_%28cropped%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Historisch_Nordamerika_%28cropped%29.jpg/300px-Historisch_Nordamerika_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Historisch_Nordamerika_%28cropped%29.jpg/400px-Historisch_Nordamerika_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" width="200"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Historisch_Nordamerika_(cropped).jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Map of North America (1621).</div></div></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Norse_colonization_of_North_America" title="Norse colonization of North America">Norse colonization of North America</a></div>
<p>There was limited <a href="/wiki/First_contact_(anthropology)" title="First contact (anthropology)">contact</a> between North American people and the outside world <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pre-Columbian_trans-oceanic_contact" title="Pre-Columbian trans-oceanic contact">before 1492</a>. Several theoretical contacts have been proposed, but the earliest physical evidence comes from the <a href="/wiki/Norsemen" title="Norsemen">Norse</a> or <a href="/wiki/Vikings" title="Vikings">Vikings</a>. <a href="/wiki/Erik_the_Red" title="Erik the Red">Erik the Red</a> founded a colony on <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">Greenland</a> in 985 CE.  Erik's son <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leif_Eriksson" title="Leif Eriksson">Leif Eriksson</a> is believed to have reached the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Island_of_Newfoundland" title="Island of Newfoundland">Island of Newfoundland</a> circa 1000, naming the discovery <a href="/wiki/Vinland" title="Vinland">Vinland</a>. The only Norse site outside of Greenland yet discovered in North America is at <a href="/wiki/L%27Anse_aux_Meadows" title="L'Anse aux Meadows">L'Anse aux Meadows</a>, Newfoundland and Labrador in Canada. All of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norse_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Norse colonization of the Americas">the Norse colonies</a> were eventually abandoned.
</p><p>The Norse voyages did not become common knowledge in the Old World. Even the permanent settlement in Greenland, which persisted until the early 1400s, received little attention and Europeans remained largely ignorant of the existence of the Americas until 1492. As part of a general <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Age_of_discovery" title="Age of discovery">age of discovery</a>, Italian sailor <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> proposed a voyage west from Europe to find a shorter route to Asia. He eventually received the backing of <a href="/wiki/Isabella_I_of_Castile" title="Isabella I of Castile">Isabella I</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_II_of_Aragon" title="Ferdinand II of Aragon">Ferdinand II</a>, Queen and King of newly united Spain. In 1492 Columbus reached land in the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">Bahamas</a>.
</p><p>Almost 500 years after the Norse, <a href="/wiki/John_Cabot" title="John Cabot">John Cabot</a> explored the east coast of what would become Canada in 1497. <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_da_Verrazzano" title="Giovanni da Verrazzano">Giovanni da Verrazzano</a> explored the <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">East Coast</a> of North America from Florida to presumably Newfoundland in 1524. <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Cartier" title="Jacques Cartier">Jacques Cartier</a> made a series of voyages on behalf of the French crown in 1534 and explored the <a href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_River" title="St. Lawrence River">St. Lawrence River</a>.
</p>

<p>To understand what constitutes successful colonization, it is important to understand what colonization means. Colonization refers to large-scale population movements in which the migrants maintain strong links with their or their ancestors' former country, gaining significant advantages over other inhabitants of the territory by such links. When colonization takes place under the protection of clearly colonial political structures, it may most handily be called <i>settler colonialism</i>. This often involves the settlers' entirely dispossessing earlier inhabitants, or instituting legal and other structures which systematically disadvantage them.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup>
</p><p>Initially, European activity consisted mostly of trade and exploration. Eventually Europeans began to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_North_American_cities_by_year_of_foundation" title="List of North American cities by year of foundation">establish settlements</a>. The three principal colonial powers in North America were <a href="/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Spanish colonization of the Americas">Spain</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="British colonization of the Americas">England</a>, and <a href="/wiki/French_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="French colonization of the Americas">France</a>, although eventually other powers such as <a href="/wiki/Dutch_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Dutch colonization of the Americas">the Netherlands</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Swedish_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Swedish colonization of the Americas">Sweden</a> also received holdings on the continent.
</p><p>Settlement by the Spanish started the <a href="/wiki/European_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="European colonization of the Americas">European colonization of the Americas</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> They gained control of most of the largest islands in the <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">Caribbean</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_Mexico" title="Spanish conquest of Mexico">conquered the Aztec empire</a>, gaining control of present-day Mexico and Central America. This was the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> in the New World. The first successful Spanish settlement in continental North America was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Veracruz,_Veracruz" title="Veracruz, Veracruz">Veracruz</a> founded by <a href="/wiki/Hern%C3%A1n_Cort%C3%A9s" title="Hernán Cortés">Hernán Cortés</a> in 1519, followed by many other settlements in colonial <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">New Spain</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Florida" title="Spanish Florida">Spanish Florida</a>, Central America, <a href="/wiki/New_Mexico" title="New Mexico">New Mexico</a>, and later California. The Spanish claimed all of North and South America (with the exception of Brazil), and no other European power challenged those claims by planting colonies until over a century after Spain's first settlements.
</p><p>The first French settlements were <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Habitation_at_Port-Royal" title="Habitation at Port-Royal">Port Royal</a> (1604) and <a href="/wiki/Quebec_City" title="Quebec City">Quebec City</a> (1608) in what is now <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">Nova Scotia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>. The <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fur_Trade" title="Fur Trade">Fur Trade</a> soon became the primary business on the continent and as a result transformed the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indigenous_North_American" title="Indigenous North American">indigenous North American</a> ways of life.
</p><p>The first permanent English settlements were at <a href="/wiki/Jamestown,_Virginia" title="Jamestown, Virginia">Jamestown</a> (1607) (along with its satellite, <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">Bermuda</a> in 1609) and <a href="/wiki/Plymouth_Colony" title="Plymouth Colony">Plymouth</a> (1620), in what are today <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">Virginia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">Massachusetts</a> respectively.
Further to the south, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Slavery_in_Colonial_America" title="Slavery in Colonial America">plantation slavery</a> became the main industry of the <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">West Indies</a>, and this gave rise to the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_slave_trade" title="Atlantic slave trade">Atlantic slave trade</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Colonial_era">Colonial era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Colonial era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Non-Native_American_Nations_Control_over_N_America_1750-2008.gif"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="783" data-file-width="700" decoding="async" height="246" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Non-Native_American_Nations_Control_over_N_America_1750-2008.gif/220px-Non-Native_American_Nations_Control_over_N_America_1750-2008.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Non-Native_American_Nations_Control_over_N_America_1750-2008.gif/330px-Non-Native_American_Nations_Control_over_N_America_1750-2008.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Non-Native_American_Nations_Control_over_N_America_1750-2008.gif/440px-Non-Native_American_Nations_Control_over_N_America_1750-2008.gif 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Non-Native_American_Nations_Control_over_N_America_1750-2008.gif" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Non-Native American Nations Control over N America 1750–2008 —<br/>in the interactive SVG version on a compatible browser, hover over the timeline to step through time</div></div></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Colonial_history_of_the_United_States" title="Colonial history of the United States">Colonial history of the United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Former_colonies_and_territories_in_Canada" title="Former colonies and territories in Canada">Former colonies and territories in Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_France" title="New France">New France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies" title="Thirteen Colonies">Thirteen Colonies</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_and_Indian_Wars" title="French and Indian Wars">French and Indian Wars</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">New Spain</a></div>
<p>By the year 1663 the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_crown" title="French crown">French crown</a> had taken over control of <a href="/wiki/New_France" title="New France">New France</a> from the fur-trading companies, and the English <a href="/wiki/Charter_colony" title="Charter colony">charter colonies</a> gave way to more metropolitan control. This ushered in a new era of more formalized colonialism in North America.
</p><p>Rivalry between the European powers created a series of wars on the North American landmass that would have great impact on the development of the colonies. Territory often changed hands multiple times. Peace was not achieved until French forces in North America were vanquished at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Plains_of_Abraham" title="Battle of the Plains of Abraham">Battle of the Plains of Abraham</a> at <a href="/wiki/Quebec_City" title="Quebec City">Quebec City</a>, and France ceded most of her claims outside of the Caribbean. The end of the French presence in North America was a disaster for most Native nations in Eastern North America, who lost their major ally against the expanding Anglo-American settlement's. During <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pontiac%27s_Rebellion" title="Pontiac's Rebellion">Pontiac's Rebellion</a> from 1763 to 1766, a confederation of Great Lakes-area tribes fought a somewhat successful campaign to defend their rights over their lands west of the <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_Mountains" title="Appalachian Mountains">Appalachian Mountains</a>, which had been "reserved" for them under the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Proclamation_of_1763" title="Royal Proclamation of 1763">Royal Proclamation of 1763</a>.
</p><p>Viceroyalty of New Spain (present-day Mexico) was the name of the <a href="/wiki/Viceroy" title="Viceroy">viceroy</a>-ruled territories of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Empire" title="Spanish Empire">Spanish Empire</a> in Asia, North America and its peripheries from 1535 to 1821.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Revolutions">Revolutions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Revolutions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/American_Revolution" title="American Revolution">American Revolution</a>, <a href="/wiki/Haitian_Revolution" title="Haitian Revolution">Haitian Revolution</a>, <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Revolution" title="Industrial Revolution">Industrial Revolution</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mexican_War_of_Independence" title="Mexican War of Independence">Mexican War of Independence</a></div>
<p>The coming of the American Revolution had a great impact across the continent. Most importantly it directly led to the creation of the United States of America. However, the associated <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a> was an important war that touched all corners of the region. The flight of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Empire_Loyalists" title="United Empire Loyalists">United Empire Loyalists</a> led to the creation of <a href="/wiki/English_Canada" title="English Canada">English Canada</a> as a separate community
</p><p>Meanwhile, Spain's hold on Mexico was weakening. Independence was declared in 1810 by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miguel_Hidalgo" title="Miguel Hidalgo">Miguel Hidalgo</a>, starting the <i>Mexican War of Independence</i>. In 1813, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mar%C3%ADa_Morelos" title="José María Morelos">José María Morelos</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Congress_of_An%C3%A1huac" title="Congress of Anáhuac">Congress of Anáhuac</a> signed the <a href="/wiki/Solemn_Act_of_the_Declaration_of_Independence_of_Northern_America" title="Solemn Act of the Declaration of Independence of Northern America">Solemn Act of the Declaration of Independence of Northern America</a>, the first legal document where the separation of the New Spain with respect to Spain is proclaimed. Spain finally recognized Mexico's independence in 1821.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion_era">Expansion era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Expansion era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_United_States_(1789%E2%80%931849)" title="History of the United States (1789–1849)">History of the United States (1789–1849)</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canada_under_British_Imperial_control_(1764%E2%80%931867)" title="Canada under British Imperial control (1764–1867)">Canada under British Imperial control (1764–1867)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mexican%E2%80%93American_War" title="Mexican–American War">Mexican–American War</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:227px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Olney%27s_1844_School_Map.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3633" data-file-width="2954" decoding="async" height="277" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Olney%27s_1844_School_Map.jpg/225px-Olney%27s_1844_School_Map.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Olney%27s_1844_School_Map.jpg/338px-Olney%27s_1844_School_Map.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Olney%27s_1844_School_Map.jpg/450px-Olney%27s_1844_School_Map.jpg 2x" width="225"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Olney%27s_1844_School_Map.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>1844 Map of North America, after the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_American_War" title="Mexican American War">Mexican American War</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>From the time of independence of the United States, that country expanded rapidly to the west, <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Purchase" title="Louisiana Purchase">acquiring the massive Louisiana territory</a> in 1803. Between 1810 and 1811 a Native confederacy under Tecumseh <a href="/wiki/Tecumseh%27s_War" title="Tecumseh's War">fought unsuccessfully</a> to keep the Americans from pushing them out of the Great Lakes. Tecumseh's followers then went north into Canada, where they helped the British to block an American attempt to seize Canada during the <a href="/wiki/War_of_1812" title="War of 1812">War of 1812</a>. Following the war, British and Irish settlement in Canada increased dramatically.
</p><p>US expansion was complicated by the division between "free" and "slave" states, which led to the <a href="/wiki/Missouri_Compromise" title="Missouri Compromise">Missouri Compromise</a> in 1820. Likewise, Canada faced a division between <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_Canadian" title="French Canadian">French</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/English_Canadian" title="English Canadian">English</a> communities that led to the outbreak of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rebellions_of_1837" title="Rebellions of 1837">civil strife in 1837</a>. Mexico faced constant political tensions between liberals and conservatives, as well as the rebellion of the English-speaking region of Texas, which declared itself the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Texas" title="Republic of Texas">Republic of Texas</a> in 1836. In 1845 Texas joined the United States, which would later lead to the <a href="/wiki/Mexican%E2%80%93American_War" title="Mexican–American War">Mexican–American War</a> in 1846 that began <a href="/wiki/American_imperialism" title="American imperialism">American imperialism</a>. As a result of conflict with Mexico, the United States made further territorial gains in <a href="/wiki/History_of_California" title="History of California">California</a> and the Southwest.
</p>
<h2><span id="Conflict.2C_confederation.2C_and_invasion"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Conflict,_confederation,_and_invasion">Conflict, confederation, and invasion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Conflict, confederation, and invasion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_United_States_(1849%E2%80%931865)" title="History of the United States (1849–1865)">History of the United States (1849–1865)</a>, <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Confederation" title="Canadian Confederation">Canadian Confederation</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/French_intervention_in_Mexico" title="French intervention in Mexico">French intervention in Mexico</a></div>
<p>The secession of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Confederate_States" title="Confederate States">Confederate States</a> and the resulting civil war rocked American society. It eventually led to the end of slavery in the United States, the destruction and later <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reconstruction_Era" title="Reconstruction Era">reconstruction</a> of most of the South, and tremendous loss of life. From the conflict, the United States emerged as a powerful industrialized nation.
</p><p>Partly as a response to the threat of American power, four of the Canadian colonies agreed to federate in 1867, creating the <a href="/wiki/Dominion" title="Dominion">Dominion</a> of Canada. The new nation was not fully sovereign, but enjoyed considerable independence from Britain. With the addition of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_Colonies_of_Vancouver_Island_and_British_Columbia" title="United Colonies of Vancouver Island and British Columbia">British Columbia</a> Canada would expand to the Pacific by 1871 and establish a transcontinental railway, the Canadian Pacific, by 1885.
</p><p>In Mexico conflicts like the <a href="/wiki/Reform_War" title="Reform War">Reform War</a> left the state weak, and open to foreign influence. This led to the <a href="/wiki/Second_French_Empire" title="Second French Empire">Second French Empire</a> to invade Mexico.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Late_19th_century">Late 19th century</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Late 19th century">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_United_States_(1865%E2%80%931918)" title="History of the United States (1865–1918)">History of the United States (1865–1918)</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reconstruction_era_of_the_United_States" title="Reconstruction era of the United States">Reconstruction era of the United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Post-Confederation_Canada_(1867%E2%80%931914)" title="Post-Confederation Canada (1867–1914)">Post-Confederation Canada (1867–1914)</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Porfiriato" title="Porfiriato">Porfiriato</a></div>
<p>In both the United States and Canada, the second half of the 19th century witnessed massive inflows of immigration to settle the West. These lands were not uninhabited however: in the United States the government fought numerous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Wars" title="Indian Wars">Indian Wars</a> against the native inhabitants. In Canada, relations were more peaceful, as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Numbered_Treaties" title="Numbered Treaties">Numbered Treaties</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Louis_Riel" title="Louis Riel">two rebellions</a> broke out in 1870 and 1885 on the prairies. The British colony of <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_Newfoundland" title="Dominion of Newfoundland">Newfoundland became a dominion</a> in 1907.
</p><p>In Mexico, the entire era was dominated by the dictatorship of <b>Porfirio Díaz</b>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="World_Wars_era">World Wars era</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: World Wars era">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Canada_in_the_World_Wars_and_Interwar_Years" title="Canada in the World Wars and Interwar Years">Canada in the World Wars and Interwar Years</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Revolution" title="Mexican Revolution">Mexican Revolution</a>, and <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_United_States_(1918%E2%80%931945)" title="History of the United States (1918–1945)">History of the United States (1918–1945)</a></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_I">World War I</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: World War I">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> and <a href="/wiki/Military_history_of_Canada_during_World_War_I" title="Military history of Canada during World War I">Military history of Canada during World War I</a></div>
<p>As a part of the <a href="/wiki/British_Empire" title="British Empire">British Empire</a> Canada immediately was at war in 1914. Canada bore the brunt of several major battles during the early stages of the war including the use of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Poison_gas_in_World_War_I" title="Poison gas in World War I">poison gas</a> attacks at <a href="/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Ypres" title="Second Battle of Ypres">Ypres</a>. Losses became grave, and the government eventually brought in <a href="/wiki/Conscription" title="Conscription">conscription</a>, despite the fact this was against the wishes of the majority of <a href="/wiki/French_Canadians" title="French Canadians">French Canadians</a>. In the ensuing <a href="/wiki/Conscription_Crisis_of_1917" title="Conscription Crisis of 1917">Conscription Crisis of 1917</a>, riots broke out on the streets of <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>. In neighboring Newfoundland, the new dominion suffered a devastating loss on July 1, 1916, the <a href="/wiki/First_day_on_the_Somme" title="First day on the Somme">First day on the Somme</a>.
</p><p>The United States stayed apart from the conflict until 1917, joining the Entente powers. The United States was then able to play a crucial role at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Paris_Peace_Conference,_1919" title="Paris Peace Conference, 1919">Paris Peace Conference of 1919</a> that shaped <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Interwar" title="Interwar">interwar</a> Europe.
</p><p>Mexico was not part of the war as the country was embroiled in the Mexican Revolution at the time.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Interwar_years">Interwar years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Interwar years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Roaring_Twenties" title="Roaring Twenties">Roaring Twenties</a>, <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression" title="Great Depression">Great Depression</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cristero_War" title="Cristero War">Cristero War</a></div>
<p>The 1920s brought an age of great prosperity in the United States, and to a lesser degree Canada. But the <a href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Crash_of_1929" title="Wall Street Crash of 1929">Wall Street Crash of 1929</a> combined with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dust_bowl" title="Dust bowl">drought</a> ushered in a period of economic hardship in the United States and <a href="/wiki/Great_Depression_in_Canada" title="Great Depression in Canada">Canada</a>.
</p><p>From 1937 to 1949, this was a popular uprising against the anti-Catholic Mexican government of the time, set off specifically by the anti-clerical provisions of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_Constitution_of_1917" title="Mexican Constitution of 1917">Mexican Constitution of 1917</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="World_War_II">World War II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: World War II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Military_history_of_Canada_during_the_Second_World_War" title="Military history of Canada during the Second World War">Military history of Canada during the Second World War</a>, <a href="/wiki/Military_history_of_the_United_States_during_World_War_II" title="Military history of the United States during World War II">Military history of the United States during World War II</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States_home_front_during_World_War_II" title="United States home front during World War II">United States home front during World War II</a>, <a href="/wiki/SS_Potrero_del_Llano" title="SS Potrero del Llano">SS Potrero del Llano</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Faja_de_Oro" title="Faja de Oro">Faja de Oro</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Escuadr%C3%B3n_201" title="Escuadrón 201">Escuadrón 201</a></div>
<p>Once again Canada found itself at war before her neighbors, however even Canadian contributions were slight before the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor" title="Attack on Pearl Harbor">attack on Pearl Harbor</a>. The entry of the United States into the war helped to tip the balance in favor of the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">Allies</a>. On August 19, 1942, a force of some 6000, largely Canadian, infantry was landed near the French channel port of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dieppe,_Seine-Maritime" title="Dieppe, Seine-Maritime">Dieppe</a>. The German defenders under General von Rundstedt destroyed the invaders. 907 Canadians were killed and almost 2,500 captured (many wounded). Lessons learned in this abortive raid were put to good use 2 years later in the successful Normandy invasion.
</p><p>Two Mexican tankers, transporting oil to the United States, were attacked and sunk by the Germans in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a> waters, in 1942. The incident happened in spite of Mexico's neutrality at that time. This led Mexico to declare war on the Axis nations and enter the conflict.
</p><p>The destruction of Europe wrought by the war vaulted all North American countries to more important roles in world affairs. The United States especially emerged as a "superpower".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-war">Post-war</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Post-war">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_United_States_(1945%E2%80%931964)" title="History of the United States (1945–1964)">History of the United States (1945–1964)</a>, <a href="/wiki/History_of_Canada_(1945%E2%80%931960)" title="History of Canada (1945–1960)">History of Canada (1945–1960)</a>, <a href="/wiki/1950s" title="1950s">1950s</a>, <a href="/wiki/1960s" title="1960s">1960s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Civil_Rights_Movement" title="Civil Rights Movement">Civil Rights Movement</a></div>
<p>The early Cold War era saw the United States as the most powerful nation in a Western coalition of which Mexico and Canada were also a part. At home, the United States witnessed convulsive change especially in the area of <a href="/wiki/Race_relations" title="Race relations">race relations</a>. In Canada this was mirrored by the <a href="/wiki/Quiet_Revolution" title="Quiet Revolution">Quiet Revolution</a> and the emergence of <a href="/wiki/Quebec_nationalism" title="Quebec nationalism">Quebec nationalism</a>.
</p><p>Mexico experienced an era of huge economic growth after World War II, a heavy industrialization process and a growth of its middle class, a period known in Mexican history as the <i>"El Milagro Mexicano"</i> (Mexican miracle).
</p><p>The Caribbean saw the beginnings of <a href="/wiki/Decolonization" title="Decolonization">decolonization</a>, while on the largest island the <a href="/wiki/Cuban_Revolution" title="Cuban Revolution">Cuban Revolution</a> introduced Cold War rivalries into Latin America.
</p><p>In 1959 the non-<a href="/wiki/Contiguous_United_States" title="Contiguous United States">contiguous</a> US territories of <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> and Hawaii became US states.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vietnam_War_and_stagflation">Vietnam War and <i>stagflation</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Vietnam War and stagflation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_United_States_(1964%E2%80%931980)" title="History of the United States (1964–1980)">History of the United States (1964–1980)</a>, <a href="/wiki/History_of_Canada_(1960%E2%80%931981)" title="History of Canada (1960–1981)">History of Canada (1960–1981)</a>, <a href="/wiki/1960s" title="1960s">1960s</a>, and <a href="/wiki/1970s" title="1970s">1970s</a></div>
<p>During this time the United States became involved in the <a href="/wiki/Vietnam_War" title="Vietnam War">Vietnam War</a> as part of the global Cold War. This war would later prove to be highly divisive in American society, and American troops were withdrawn from Indochina in 1975 with the <a href="/wiki/Khmer_Rouge" title="Khmer Rouge">Khmer Rouge</a>'s capture of <a href="/wiki/Phnom_Penh" title="Phnom Penh">Phnom Penh</a> on April 17, the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vietnam_People%27s_Army" title="Vietnam People's Army">Vietnam People's Army</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fall_of_Saigon" title="Fall of Saigon">capture</a> of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saigon" title="Saigon">Saigon</a> on April 30 and the <a href="/wiki/Pathet_Lao" title="Pathet Lao">Pathet Lao</a>'s capture of <a href="/wiki/Vientiane" title="Vientiane">Vientiane</a> on December 2.
</p><p>Canada during this era was dominated by the leadership of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pierre_Elliot_Trudeau" title="Pierre Elliot Trudeau">Pierre Elliot Trudeau</a>. Eventually in 1982 at the end of his tenure, Canada received a new constitution.
</p><p>Both the United States and Canada experienced <a href="/wiki/Stagflation" title="Stagflation">stagflation</a>, which eventually led to a revival in small-government politics.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2007)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_1980s">The 1980s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: The 1980s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/1980s" title="1980s">1980s</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_the_United_States_(1980%E2%80%931988)" title="History of the United States (1980–1988)">History of the United States (1980–1988)</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_Canada_(1982%E2%80%931992)" title="History of Canada (1982–1992)">History of Canada (1982–1992)</a></div>
<p>Mexican presidents <a href="/wiki/Miguel_de_la_Madrid" title="Miguel de la Madrid">Miguel de la Madrid</a>, in the early 1980s and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Salinas_de_Gortari" title="Carlos Salinas de Gortari">Carlos Salinas de Gortari</a> in the late 1980s, started implementing liberal economic strategies that were seen as a good move. However, Mexico experienced a strong economic recession in 1982 and the Mexican peso suffered a devaluation. Presidential elections held in 1988 were forecast to be very competitive and they were. Leftist candidate <a href="/wiki/Cuauht%C3%A9moc_C%C3%A1rdenas" title="Cuauhtémoc Cárdenas">Cuauhtémoc Cárdenas</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A1zaro_C%C3%A1rdenas" title="Lázaro Cárdenas">Lázaro Cárdenas</a> one of the most beloved Mexican presidents, created a successful campaign and was reported as the leader in several opinion polls. On July 6, 1988, the day of the elections, a system shutdown of the <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM AS/400</a> that the government was using to count the votes occurred, presumably by accident. The government simply stated that <i>"se cayó el sistema"</i> ("the system crashed"), to refer to the incident. When the system was finally restored, the PRI candidate Carlos Salinas was declared the official winner. It was the first time since the Revolution that a non-PRI candidate was so close to winning the presidency.
</p><p>In the United States president <a href="/wiki/Ronald_Reagan" title="Ronald Reagan">Ronald Reagan</a> attempted to move the United States back towards a hard anti-communist line in foreign affairs, in what his supporters saw as an attempt to assert moral leadership (compared to the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>) in the world community. Domestically, Reagan attempted to bring in a package of <a href="/wiki/Privatization" title="Privatization">privatization</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trickle_down_economics" title="Trickle down economics">trickle down economics</a> to stimulate the economy.
</p><p>Canada's <a href="/wiki/Brian_Mulroney" title="Brian Mulroney">Brian Mulroney</a> ran on a similar platform to Reagan, and also favored closer trade ties with the United States. This led to the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canada-United_States_Free_Trade_Agreement" title="Canada-United States Free Trade Agreement">Canada-United States Free Trade Agreement</a> in January 1989.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_history">Recent history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=History_of_North_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Recent history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/1990s" title="1990s">1990s</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_the_United_States_(since_1988)" title="History of the United States (since 1988)">History of the United States (since 1988)</a>, and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_Canada_(1992%E2%80%93present)" title="History of Canada (1992–present)">History of Canada (1992–present)</a></div>
<p>The End of the Cold War and the beginning of the era of sustained economic expansion coincided during the 1990s. On January 1, 1994, Canada, Mexico, and the United States signed the <a href="/wiki/North_American_Free_Trade_Agreement" title="North American Free Trade Agreement">North American Free Trade Agreement</a>, creating the world's largest free trade area. Quebec held a referendum in 1995 for national sovereignty in which 51% voted no to 49% yes. In 2000, <a href="/wiki/Vicente_Fox" title="Vicente Fox">Vicente Fox</a> became the first non-<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Partido_Revolucionario_Institucional" title="Partido Revolucionario Institucional">PRI</a> candidate to win the Mexican presidency in over 70 years.
</p><p>The optimism of the 1990s was shattered by the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/9/11" title="9/11">9/11</a> attacks of 2001 on the United States, which <a href="/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_(2001%E2%80%932021)" title="War in Afghanistan (2001–2021)">prompted the 20-year period of military intervention war</a> in <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>, which Canada also participated in. Meanwhile, Canada and Mexico both did not support the United States later moves to <a href="/wiki/Iraq_War" title="Iraq War">invade</a> <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a>.
</p><p>In 2006, the drug war in Mexico evolved into an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_Drug_War" title="Mexican Drug War">actual military conflict</a> with each year more deadly than the last.
</p><p>Starting in early 2008, the <a href="/wiki/Great_Recession" title="Great Recession">Great Recession</a> in 3 North American nations began, which eventually triggered a worldwide recession in the Fall of 2008 and still has not recovered until 2009. In 2009, <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a> was inaugurated as the first African American to be President of the United States. 2 years later, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Osama_Bin_Laden" title="Osama Bin Laden">Osama Bin Laden</a>, the perpetrator of 9/11, was <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Death_of_Osama_Bin_Laden" title="Death of Osama Bin Laden">found and killed</a>. On December 18, 2011, the <a href="/wiki/Iraq_War" title="Iraq War">Iraq War</a> was declared formally over once the troops had <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Withdrawal_of_U.S._troops_from_Iraq_(2007%E2%80%932011)" title="Withdrawal of U.S. troops from Iraq (2007–2011)">pulled out</a>.
</p><p>In 2016, <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> was elected 45th President, who defeated <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">Hillary Clinton</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2016_United_States_presidential_election" title="2016 United States presidential election">2016 election</a>, and in 2017, he was sworn in as president. In early 2020, the United States confirmed the first case of the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_the_United_States" title="COVID-19 pandemic in the United States">novel coronavirus</a>, and later that month in March, North America went into national lockdown as the whole region was affected by the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_North_America" title="COVID-19 pandemic in North America">COVID-19 pandemic</a>. As of December 2021, three North American nations have suffered more than 1.3 million COVID-19 related deaths.
</p><p>In 2020, <a href="/wiki/Joe_Biden" title="Joe Biden">Joe Biden</a> was elected 46th President, who defeated <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">Donald Trump</a> in <a href="/wiki/2020_United_States_presidential_election" title="2020 United States presidential election">2020 presidential election</a>, although Trump refused to concede the election, and on January 6, 2021, a group of his supporters <a href="/wiki/January_6_United_States_Capitol_attack" title="January 6 United States Capitol attack">stormed the United States Capitol</a> in an <a href="/wiki/Attempts_to_overturn_the_2020_United_States_presidential_election" title="Attempts to overturn the 2020 United States presidential election">unsuccessful effort</a> to disrupt the <a href="/wiki/2021_United_States_Electoral_College_vote_count" title="2021 United States Electoral College vote count">presidential Electoral College vote count</a>.
</p><p>Also, Canadian Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Justin_Trudeau" title="Justin Trudeau">Justin Trudeau</a> called a <a href="/wiki/Snap_election" title="Snap election">snap election</a> in 2021 to try and increase the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Canada" title="Liberal Party of Canada">Liberals</a> seat share and reach a majority government. The results of <a href="/wiki/2021_Canadian_federal_election" title="2021 Canadian federal election">2021 federal election</a> were mostly unchanged from <a href="/wiki/2019_Canadian_federal_election" title="2019 Canadian federal election">2019 federal election</a>, with the Liberals forming another minority government.
</p>
