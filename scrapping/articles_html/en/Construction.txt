<p><b>Construction</b> is a general term meaning the <a href="/wiki/Art" title="Art">art</a> and <a href="/wiki/Science" title="Science">science</a> to form <a href="/wiki/Physical_object" title="Physical object">objects</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Systems" title="Systems">systems</a>, or <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Organizations" title="Organizations">organizations</a>,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OED_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-OED-1">[1]</a></sup> and comes from <a href="/wiki/Latin" title="Latin">Latin</a> <i>constructio</i> (from <i>com-</i> "together" and <i>struere</i> "to pile up") and <a href="/wiki/Old_French" title="Old French">Old French</a> <i>construction</i>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> To construct is the <a href="/wiki/Verb" title="Verb">verb</a>: the act of building, and the <a href="/wiki/Noun" title="Noun">noun</a> is construction: how something is built, the nature of its structure.
</p><p>In its most widely used context, construction covers the processes involved in delivering <a href="/wiki/Building" title="Building">buildings</a>, <a href="/wiki/Infrastructure" title="Infrastructure">infrastructure</a>, industrial facilities and associated activities through to the end of their life. It typically starts with <a href="/wiki/Planning" title="Planning">planning</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Financing" title="Financing">financing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Design" title="Design">design</a>, and continues until the asset is built and ready for use; construction also covers repairs and maintenance work, any works to expand, extend and improve the asset, and its eventual <a href="/wiki/Demolition" title="Demolition">demolition</a>, dismantling or <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/decommission" title="wikt:decommission">decommissioning</a>.
</p><p>The construction industry contributes significantly to many countries’ gross domestic products (GDP). Global expenditure on construction activities was about $4 trillion in 2012. Today, expenditure on the construction industry exceeds $11 trillion a year, equivalent to about 13 percent of global <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">GDP</a>. This spending was forecast to rise to around $14.8 trillion in 2030.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCP_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-GCP-3">[3]</a></sup>
</p><p>Although the construction industry promotes economic development and brings many non-monetary benefits to many countries, it is one of the most hazardous industries. For example, about 20% (1,061) of US industry fatalities in 2019 happened in construction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OSHA_stats_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-OSHA_stats-4">[4]</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/History_of_construction" title="History of construction">History of construction</a></div>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/History_of_architecture" title="History of architecture">History of architecture</a></div>
<p>The first <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hut_(dwelling)" title="Hut (dwelling)">huts</a> and shelters were constructed by hand or with simple tools. As <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cities" title="Cities">cities</a> grew during the <a href="/wiki/Bronze_Age" title="Bronze Age">Bronze Age</a>, a class of professional <a href="/wiki/Artisan" title="Artisan">craftsmen</a>, like <a href="/wiki/Bricklayer" title="Bricklayer">bricklayers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carpentry" title="Carpentry">carpenters</a>, appeared. Occasionally, <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slaves</a> were used for construction work. In the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a>, the artisan craftsmen were organized into <a href="/wiki/Guild" title="Guild">guilds</a>. In the 19th century, steam-powered machinery appeared, and, later, diesel- and electric-powered vehicles such as <a href="/wiki/Crane_(machine)" title="Crane (machine)">cranes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Excavator" title="Excavator">excavators</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bulldozer" title="Bulldozer">bulldozers</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Fast-track_construction" title="Fast-track construction">Fast-track construction</a> has been increasingly popular in the 21st century. Some estimates suggest that 40% of construction projects are now fast-track construction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_industry_sectors">Construction industry sectors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Construction industry sectors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Industrial-installation.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="676" data-file-width="1203" decoding="async" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Industrial-installation.jpg/220px-Industrial-installation.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Industrial-installation.jpg/330px-Industrial-installation.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Industrial-installation.jpg/440px-Industrial-installation.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Industrial-installation.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Industrial assemblage of a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thermal_oxidiser" title="Thermal oxidiser">thermal oxidizer</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States of America</a></div></div></div>
<p>Broadly, there are three sectors of construction: buildings, infrastructure and industrial:<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Building construction is usually further divided into residential and non-residential.</li>
<li>Infrastructure, also called <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineering" title="Civil engineering">heavy civil or heavy engineering</a>, includes large public works, dams, bridges, highways, railways, water or wastewater and utility distribution.</li>
<li>Industrial construction includes <a href="/wiki/Offshore_construction" title="Offshore construction">offshore construction</a> (mainly of energy installations), mining and quarrying, <a href="/wiki/Refining" title="Refining">refineries</a>, chemical processing, <a href="/wiki/Electricity_generation" title="Electricity generation">power generation</a>, mills and <a href="/wiki/Factory" title="Factory">manufacturing plants</a>.</li></ul>
<p>The industry can also be classified into sectors or markets.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> For example, <i><a href="/wiki/Engineering_News-Record" title="Engineering News-Record">Engineering News-Record</a></i> (<i>ENR</i>), a US-based construction trade magazine, has compiled and reported data about the size of design and construction contractors. In 2014, it split the data into nine market segments: transportation, <a href="/wiki/Petroleum_industry" title="Petroleum industry">petroleum</a>, buildings, power, industrial, water, manufacturing, sewer/waste, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telecommunication" title="Telecommunication">telecom</a>, hazardous waste, and a tenth category for other projects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> <i>ENR</i> used data on transportation, sewer, hazardous waste and water to rank firms as heavy contractors.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Standard_Industrial_Classification" title="Standard Industrial Classification">Standard Industrial Classification</a> and the newer <a href="/wiki/North_American_Industry_Classification_System" title="North American Industry Classification System">North American Industry Classification System</a> classify companies that perform or engage in construction into three subsectors: building construction, heavy and civil engineering construction, and specialty trade contractors. There are also categories for professional services firms (e.g., <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a>, <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Surveying" title="Surveying">surveying</a>, <a href="/wiki/Project_management" title="Project management">project management</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Building construction is the process of adding structures to areas of land, also known as <a href="/wiki/Real_property" title="Real property">real property</a> sites. Typically, a project is instigated by or with the owner of the property (who may be an individual or an organisation); occasionally, land may be <a href="/wiki/Eminent_domain" title="Eminent domain">compulsorily purchased</a> from the owner for public use.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Residential_construction">Residential construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Residential construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Home_construction" title="Home construction">Home construction</a></div>
<p>Residential construction may be undertaken by individual land-owners (<a href="/wiki/Self-build" title="Self-build">self-build</a>), by specialist <a href="/wiki/Home_construction" title="Home construction">house-builders</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Real_estate_development" title="Real estate development">property developers</a>, by <a href="/wiki/General_contractor" title="General contractor">general contractors</a>, or by providers of <a href="/wiki/Public_housing" title="Public housing">public or social housing</a> (e.g.: local authorities, <a href="/wiki/Housing_association" title="Housing association">housing associations</a>). Where local <a href="/wiki/Zoning" title="Zoning">zoning</a> or <a href="/wiki/Planning_permission" title="Planning permission">planning</a> policies allow, <a href="/wiki/Mixed-use_development" title="Mixed-use development">mixed-use developments</a> may comprise both residential and non-residential construction (e.g.: retail, leisure, offices, public buildings, etc.).
</p><p>Residential construction practices, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Technologies" title="Technologies">technologies</a>, and resources must conform to local building <a href="/wiki/Authority" title="Authority">authority</a> regulations and <a href="/wiki/Building_code" title="Building code">codes of practice</a>. Materials readily available in the area generally dictate the construction materials used (e.g.: <a href="/wiki/Brick" title="Brick">brick</a> versus stone versus <a href="/wiki/Lumber" title="Lumber">timber</a>). Costs of construction on a per square meter (or per square foot) basis for <a href="/wiki/House" title="House">houses</a> can vary dramatically based on site conditions, access routes, local regulations, <a href="/wiki/Economies_of_scale" title="Economies of scale">economies of scale</a> (custom-designed homes are often more expensive to build) and the availability of skilled tradespeople.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Non-residential_construction">Non-residential construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Non-residential construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Liberty_Memorial_043.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1704" data-file-width="2272" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Liberty_Memorial_043.jpg/220px-Liberty_Memorial_043.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Liberty_Memorial_043.jpg/330px-Liberty_Memorial_043.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Liberty_Memorial_043.jpg/440px-Liberty_Memorial_043.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Liberty_Memorial_043.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Construction of the Federal Reserve building in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a></div></div></div>
<p>Depending upon the <a href="/wiki/List_of_building_types" title="List of building types">type of building</a>, non-residential building construction can be procured by a wide range of private and public organisations, including local authorities, educational and religious bodies, transport undertakings, retailers, hoteliers, property developers, financial institutions and other private companies. Most construction in these sectors is undertaken by general <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contractors" title="Contractors">contractors</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Infrastructure_construction">Infrastructure construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Infrastructure construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineering" title="Civil engineering">Civil engineering</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shasta_dam_under_construction_new_edit.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4498" data-file-width="6022" decoding="async" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Shasta_dam_under_construction_new_edit.jpg/220px-Shasta_dam_under_construction_new_edit.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Shasta_dam_under_construction_new_edit.jpg/330px-Shasta_dam_under_construction_new_edit.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Shasta_dam_under_construction_new_edit.jpg/440px-Shasta_dam_under_construction_new_edit.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shasta_dam_under_construction_new_edit.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Shasta_Dam" title="Shasta Dam">Shasta Dam</a> under construction in June 1942</div></div></div>
<p>Civil engineering covers the design, construction, and maintenance of the physical and naturally built environment, including public works such as roads, bridges, canals, dams, tunnels, airports, water and sewerage systems, pipelines, and railways.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> Some general contractors have expertise in civil engineering; civil engineering contractors are firms dedicated to work in this sector, and may specialise in particular types of infrastructure.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_construction">Industrial construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Industrial construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Factory_of_National_Cement_Share_Company.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3744" data-file-width="5616" decoding="async" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Factory_of_National_Cement_Share_Company.jpg/220px-Factory_of_National_Cement_Share_Company.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Factory_of_National_Cement_Share_Company.jpg/330px-Factory_of_National_Cement_Share_Company.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Factory_of_National_Cement_Share_Company.jpg/440px-Factory_of_National_Cement_Share_Company.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Factory_of_National_Cement_Share_Company.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The National Cement Share Company of <a href="/wiki/Ethiopia" title="Ethiopia">Ethiopia</a>'s new plant in <a href="/wiki/Dire_Dawa" title="Dire Dawa">Dire Dawa</a></div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Industrial_architecture" title="Industrial architecture">Industrial construction</a> includes offshore construction (mainly of energy installations: <a href="/wiki/Oil_platform" title="Oil platform">oil and gas platforms</a>, <a href="/wiki/Offshore_wind_power" title="Offshore wind power">wind power</a>), <a href="/wiki/Mining" title="Mining">mining</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quarry" title="Quarry">quarrying</a>, <a href="/wiki/Refinery" title="Refinery">refineries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brewery" title="Brewery">breweries</a>, <a href="/wiki/Distillation" title="Distillation">distilleries</a> and other processing plants, <a href="/wiki/Power_station" title="Power station">power stations</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steel_mill" title="Steel mill">steel mills</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warehouse" title="Warehouse">warehouses</a> and <a href="/wiki/Factory" title="Factory">factories</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_processes">Construction processes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Construction processes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some construction projects are small renovations or repair jobs, like repainting or fixing leaks, where the owner may act as designer, paymaster and laborer for the entire project. However, more complex or ambitious projects usually require additional multi-disciplinary expertise and manpower, so the owner may commission one or more specialist businesses to undertake detailed planning, design, construction and handover of the work. Often the owner will appoint one business to oversee the project (this may be a <a href="/wiki/Designer" title="Designer">designer</a>, a <a href="/wiki/General_contractor" title="General contractor">contractor</a>, a <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Construction_manager" title="Construction manager">construction manager</a>, or other advisors); such specialists are normally appointed for their expertise in project delivery, and will help the owner define the project <a href="/wiki/Brief_(architecture)" title="Brief (architecture)">brief</a>, agree on a <a href="/wiki/Budget" title="Budget">budget</a> and <a href="/wiki/Schedule" title="Schedule">schedule</a>, liaise with relevant public authorities, and procure the services of other specialists (the <a href="/wiki/Supply_chain" title="Supply chain">supply chain</a>, comprising <a href="/wiki/Subcontractor" title="Subcontractor">subcontractors</a>). <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Contracts" title="Contracts">Contracts</a> are agreed for the delivery of services by all businesses, alongside other detailed plans aimed at ensuring legal, timely, on-budget and safe delivery of the specified works.
</p><p>Design, finance, and legal aspects overlap and interrelate. The design must be not only structurally sound and appropriate for the use and location, but must also be financially possible to build, and legal to use. The financial structure must be adequate to build the design provided, and must pay amounts that are legally owed. Legal structures integrate design with other activities, and enforce financial and other construction processes.
</p><p>These processes also affect procurement strategies. Clients may, for example, appoint a business to design the project, after which a competitive process is undertaken to appoint a lead contractor to construct the asset (<a href="/wiki/Design%E2%80%93bid%E2%80%93build" title="Design–bid–build">design–bid–build</a>); they may appoint a business to lead both design and construction (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Design-build" title="Design-build">design-build</a>); or they may directly appoint a designer, contractor and specialist subcontractors (<a href="/wiki/Construction_management" title="Construction management">construction management</a>).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Some forms of procurement emphasize collaborative relationships (<a href="/wiki/Construction_partnering" title="Construction partnering">partnering</a>, alliancing) between the client, the contractor, and other stakeholders within a construction project, seeking to ameliorate often highly competitive and adversarial industry practices.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Planning">Planning</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Planning">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main articles: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Architectural_plan" title="Architectural plan">Architectural plan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pre-construction_services" title="Pre-construction services">Pre-construction services</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mall_culture_jakarta57.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1920" data-file-width="2560" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Mall_culture_jakarta57.jpg/220px-Mall_culture_jakarta57.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Mall_culture_jakarta57.jpg/330px-Mall_culture_jakarta57.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/Mall_culture_jakarta57.jpg/440px-Mall_culture_jakarta57.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mall_culture_jakarta57.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Digging the foundation for a building construction in <a href="/wiki/Jakarta" title="Jakarta">Jakarta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a></div></div></div>
<p>When applicable, a proposed construction project must comply with local <a href="/wiki/Land-use_planning" title="Land-use planning">land-use planning</a> policies including <a href="/wiki/Zoning" title="Zoning">zoning</a> and <a href="/wiki/Building_code" title="Building code">building code</a> requirements. A project will normally be assessed (by the 'authority having jurisdiction, AHJ, typically the <a href="/wiki/Municipality" title="Municipality">municipality</a> where the project will be located) for its potential impacts on neighbouring properties, and upon existing infrastructure (transportation, social infrastructure, and utilities including water supply, sewerage, electricity, telecommunications, etc.). Data may be gathered through <a href="/wiki/Site_analysis" title="Site analysis">site analysis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Site_survey" title="Site survey">site surveys</a> and <a href="/wiki/Geotechnical_investigation" title="Geotechnical investigation">geotechnical investigations</a>. Construction normally cannot start until <a href="/wiki/Planning_permission" title="Planning permission">planning permission</a> has been granted, and may require preparatory work to ensure relevant infrastructure has been upgraded before building work can commence. Preparatory works will also include surveys of existing utility lines to avoid damage-causing outages and other hazardous situations.
</p><p>Some legal requirements come from <i><a href="/wiki/Malum_in_se" title="Malum in se">malum in se</a></i> considerations, or the desire to prevent indisputably bad phenomena, e.g. explosions or bridge collapses. Other legal requirements come from <i><a href="/wiki/Malum_prohibitum" title="Malum prohibitum">malum prohibitum</a></i> considerations, or factors that are a matter of custom or expectation, such as isolating businesses from a business district or residences from a residential district. An attorney may seek changes or exemptions in the law that governs the land where the building will be built, either by arguing that a rule is inapplicable (the bridge design will not cause a collapse), or that the custom is no longer needed (acceptance of live-work spaces has grown in the community).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup>
</p><p>During the construction of a building, a municipal building inspector usually inspects the ongoing work periodically to ensure that construction adheres to the approved plans and the local building code. Once construction is complete, any later changes made to a building or other asset that affect safety, including its use, expansion, structural integrity, and <a href="/wiki/Fire_protection" title="Fire protection">fire protection</a>, usually require municipality approval.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Finance">Finance</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Finance">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Depending on the type of project, <a href="/wiki/Mortgage_bank" title="Mortgage bank">mortgage bankers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Accountant" title="Accountant">accountants</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cost_engineering" title="Cost engineering">cost engineers</a> may participate in creating an overall plan for the financial management of a construction project. The presence of the mortgage banker is highly likely, even in relatively small projects since the owner's equity in the property is the most obvious source of funding for a building project. Accountants act to study the expected monetary flow over the life of the project and to monitor the payouts throughout the process. Professionals including cost engineers, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Estimators" title="Estimators">estimators</a> and <a href="/wiki/Quantity_surveyor" title="Quantity surveyor">quantity surveyors</a> apply expertise to relate the work and materials involved to a proper valuation.
</p><p>Financial planning ensures adequate safeguards and contingency plans are in place before the project is started, and ensures that the plan is properly executed over the life of the project. Construction projects can suffer from preventable financial problems.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Underbids happen when builders ask for too little money to complete the project. <a href="/wiki/Cash_flow" title="Cash flow">Cash flow</a> problems exist when the present amount of funding cannot cover the current costs for labour and materials; such problems may arise even when the overall budget is adequate, presenting a temporary issue. Cost overruns with government projects have occurred when the contractor identified change orders or project changes that increased costs, which are not subject to competition from other firms as they have already been eliminated from consideration after the initial bid.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fraud" title="Fraud">Fraud</a> is also an occasional construction issue.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup>
</p><p>Large projects can involve highly complex financial plans and often start with a conceptual <a href="/wiki/Cost_estimate" title="Cost estimate">cost estimate</a> performed by a <a href="/wiki/Building_estimator" title="Building estimator">building estimator</a>. As portions of a project are completed, they may be sold, supplanting one lender or owner for another, while the logistical requirements of having the right trades and materials available for each stage of the building construction project carry forward. <a href="/wiki/Public%E2%80%93private_partnership" title="Public–private partnership">Public–private partnerships</a> (PPPs) or <a href="/wiki/Private_finance_initiative" title="Private finance initiative">private finance initiatives</a> (PFIs) may also be used to help deliver major projects. According to <a href="/wiki/McKinsey_%26_Company" title="McKinsey &amp; Company">McKinsey</a> in 2019, the "vast majority of large construction projects go over budget and take 20% longer than expected".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Legal">Legal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Legal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1097763485" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><table class="box-Unreferenced_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Unreferenced" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div class="mbox-image-div"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg"><img alt="" data-file-height="399" data-file-width="512" decoding="async" height="39" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" width="50"/></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>does not <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">cite</a> any <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">sources</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit">improve this section</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Referencing_for_beginners" title="Help:Referencing for beginners">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. <link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Construction_law" title="Construction law">Construction law</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:401-403-410_and_Construction_crop.png"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1645" data-file-width="2226" decoding="async" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/401-403-410_and_Construction_crop.png/220px-401-403-410_and_Construction_crop.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/401-403-410_and_Construction_crop.png/330px-401-403-410_and_Construction_crop.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/401-403-410_and_Construction_crop.png/440px-401-403-410_and_Construction_crop.png 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:401-403-410_and_Construction_crop.png" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Construction along <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Highway_401" title="Ontario Highway 401">Ontario Highway 401</a>, widening the road from six to twelve travel lanes</div></div></div>
<p>A construction project is a complex net of <a href="/wiki/Construction_contract" title="Construction contract">construction contracts</a> and other legal obligations, each of which all parties must carefully consider. A contract is the exchange of a set of obligations between two or more parties, and provides structures to manage issues. For example, construction delays can be costly, so construction contracts set out clear expectations and clear paths to manage delays. Poorly drafted contracts can lead to confusion and costly disputes.
</p><p>At the start of a project, legal advisors seek to identify ambiguities and other potential sources of trouble in the contract structures, and to present options for preventing problems. During projects, they work to avoid and resolve conflicts that arise. In each case, the lawyer facilitates an exchange of obligations that matches the reality of the project.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Procurement">Procurement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Procurement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Traditional_or_Design-bid-build">Traditional or Design-bid-build</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Traditional or Design-bid-build">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Design%E2%80%93bid%E2%80%93build" title="Design–bid–build">Design–bid–build</a></div>
<p>Design-bid-build is the most common and well-established method of construction procurement. In this arrangement, the <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a>, <a href="/wiki/Engineer" title="Engineer">engineer</a> or builder acts for the client as the project coordinator. They design the works, prepare specifications and design deliverables (models, drawings, etc.), administer the contract, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Request_for_tender" title="Request for tender">tender</a> the works, and manage the works from inception to completion. In parallel, there are direct contractual links between the client and the main contractor, who, in turn, has direct contractual relationships with subcontractors. The arrangement continues until the project is ready for handover.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Design-build">Design-build</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Design-build">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Design-build" title="Design-build">Design-build</a></div>
<p>Design-build became more common from the late 20th century, and involves the client contracting a single entity to provide design and construction. In some cases, the design-build package can also include finding the site, arranging funding and applying for all necessary statutory consents. Typically, the client invites several D&amp;B contractors to submit proposals to meet the project brief and then selects a preferred supplier. Often this will be a <a href="/wiki/Consortium" title="Consortium">consortium</a> involving a design firm and a contractor (sometimes more than one of each). In the United States, <a href="/wiki/Department_of_transportation" title="Department of transportation">departments of transportation</a> usually use design-build contracts as a way of progressing projects where states lack the skills or resources, particularly for very large projects.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cronin_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-Cronin-20">[20]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Construction_management">Construction management</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Construction management">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Construction_management" title="Construction management">Construction management</a></div>
<p>In a construction management arrangement, the client enters into separate contracts with the designer (architect or engineer), a <a href="/wiki/Construction_management" title="Construction management">construction manager</a>, and individual <a href="/wiki/Tradesman" title="Tradesman">trade contractors</a>. The client takes on the contractual role, while the construction or project manager provides the active role of managing the separate trade contracts, and ensuring that they complete all work smoothly and effectively together. This approach is often used to speed up procurement processes, to allow the client greater flexibility in design variation throughout the contract, to enable the appointment of individual work contractors, to separate contractual responsibility on each individual throughout the contract, and to provide greater client control.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the industrialized world, construction usually involves the translation of designs into reality. Most commonly (ie: in a design-bid-build project), the design team is employed by (i.e. in contract with) the property owner. Depending upon the type of project, a design team may include <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architects</a>, <a href="/wiki/Civil_engineer" title="Civil engineer">civil engineers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mechanical_engineering" title="Mechanical engineering">mechanical engineers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Structural_engineer" title="Structural engineer">structural engineers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fire_protection_engineering" title="Fire protection engineering">fire protection engineers</a>, planning <a href="/wiki/Consultant" title="Consultant">consultants</a>, architectural consultants, and archaeological consultants. A 'lead designer' will normally be identified to help coordinate different disciplinary inputs to the overall design. This may be aided by integration of previously separate disciplines (often undertaken by separate firms) into multi-disciplinary firms with experts from all related fields,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup> or by firms establishing relationships to support design-build processes.
</p><p>The increasing complexity of construction projects creates the need for design professionals trained in all phases of a project's life-cycle and develop an appreciation of the asset as an advanced technological system requiring close integration of many sub-systems and their individual components, including sustainability. For buildings, <a href="/wiki/Architectural_engineering" title="Architectural engineering">building engineering</a> is an emerging discipline that attempts to meet this new challenge.
</p><p>Traditionally, design has involved the production of <a href="/wiki/Sketch_(drawing)" title="Sketch (drawing)">sketches</a>, <a href="/wiki/Architectural_drawing" title="Architectural drawing">architectural</a> and <a href="/wiki/Engineering_drawing" title="Engineering drawing">engineering drawings</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Specification_(technical_standard)" title="Specification (technical standard)">specifications</a>. Until the late 20th century, drawings were largely hand-<a href="/wiki/Drafter" title="Drafter">drafted</a>; adoption of <a href="/wiki/Computer-aided_design" title="Computer-aided design">computer-aided design</a> (CAD) technologies then improved design productivity, while the 21st-century introduction of <a href="/wiki/Building_information_modeling" title="Building information modeling">building information modeling</a> (BIM) processes has involved the use of computer-generated models that can be used in their own right or to generate drawings and other visualisations as well as capturing non-geometric data about building components and systems.
</p><p>On some projects, work on-site will not start until design work is largely complete; on others, some design work may be undertaken concurrently with the early stages of on-site activity (for example, work on a building's foundations may commence while designers are still working on the detailed designs of the building's internal spaces). Some projects may include elements that are designed for <a href="/wiki/Off-site_construction" title="Off-site construction">off-site construction</a> (see also <a href="/wiki/Prefabrication" title="Prefabrication">prefabrication</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modular_building" title="Modular building">modular building</a>) and are then delivered to the site ready for erection, installation or assembly.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="On-site_construction">On-site construction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: On-site construction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:-37_Construction.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="9967" data-file-width="12528" decoding="async" height="175" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/-37_Construction.jpg/220px--37_Construction.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/-37_Construction.jpg/330px--37_Construction.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/-37_Construction.jpg/440px--37_Construction.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:-37_Construction.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>On-site <a href="/wiki/Foundation_(engineering)" title="Foundation (engineering)">foundation construction</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Once contractors and other relevant professionals have been appointed and designs are sufficiently advanced, work may commence on the project site. Typically, a construction site will include a secure perimeter to restrict unauthorised access, site access control points, office and welfare accommodation for personnel from the main contractor and other firms involved in the project team, and storage areas for materials, machinery and equipment. According to the <i>McGraw-Hill Dictionary of Architecture and Construction's</i> definition, construction may be said to have <i>started</i> when the first feature of the permanent structure has been put in place, such as pile driving, or the pouring of slabs or footings.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commissioning_and_handover">Commissioning and handover</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Commissioning and handover">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/New-construction_building_commissioning" title="New-construction building commissioning">New-construction building commissioning</a></div>
<p>Commissioning is the process of verifying that all subsystems of a new building (or other assets) work as intended to achieve the owner's project requirements and as designed by the project's architects and engineers.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Defects_liability_period">Defects liability period</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Defects liability period">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A period after handover (or practical completion) during which the owner may identify any shortcomings in relation to the building specification ('defects'), with a view to the contractor correcting the defect.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23"><a href="#cite_note-23">[23]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Maintenance.2C_repair_and_improvement"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Maintenance,_repair_and_improvement">Maintenance, repair and improvement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Maintenance, repair and improvement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Maintenance_(technical)" title="Maintenance (technical)">Maintenance (technical)</a></div>
<p>Maintenance involves functional checks, servicing, repairing or replacing of necessary devices, equipment, <a href="/wiki/Machine" title="Machine">machinery</a>, building infrastructure, and supporting utilities in industrial, business, governmental, and residential installations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Demolition">Demolition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Demolition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Demolition" title="Demolition">Demolition</a></div>
<p>Demolition is the discipline of safely and efficiently tearing down <a href="/wiki/Building" title="Building">buildings</a> and other artificial <a href="/wiki/Structure" title="Structure">structures</a>. Demolition contrasts with <a href="/wiki/Deconstruction_(building)" title="Deconstruction (building)">deconstruction</a>, which involves taking a building apart while carefully preserving valuable elements for reuse purposes (<a href="/wiki/Recycling" title="Recycling">recycling</a> - see also <a href="/wiki/Circular_economy" title="Circular economy">circular economy</a>).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry_scale_and_characteristics">Industry scale and characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Industry scale and characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_activity">Economic activity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Economic activity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:European_Antennas_Under_Construction.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2758" data-file-width="4194" decoding="async" height="145" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/European_Antennas_Under_Construction.jpg/220px-European_Antennas_Under_Construction.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/European_Antennas_Under_Construction.jpg/330px-European_Antennas_Under_Construction.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/European_Antennas_Under_Construction.jpg/440px-European_Antennas_Under_Construction.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:European_Antennas_Under_Construction.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Helicopter view of the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (<a href="/wiki/Atacama_Large_Millimeter_Array" title="Atacama Large Millimeter Array">ALMA</a>) Operations Support Facility (OSF) construction site</div></div></div>
<p>The output of the global construction industry was worth an estimated $10.8 trillion in 2017, and in 2018 was forecast to rise to $12.9 trillion by 2022,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BDC-08Oct2018_26-0"><a href="#cite_note-BDC-08Oct2018-26">[26]</a></sup> and to around $14.8 trillion in 2030.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GCP_3-1"><a href="#cite_note-GCP-3">[3]</a></sup> As a sector, construction accounts for more than 10% of global GDP (in <a href="/wiki/Developed_country" title="Developed country">developed countries</a>, construction comprises 6-9% of GDP),<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> and employs around 7% of the total employed workforce around the globe<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Potensis_28-0"><a href="#cite_note-Potensis-28">[28]</a></sup> (accounting for over 273 million full- and part-time jobs in 2014).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ICED_29-0"><a href="#cite_note-ICED-29">[29]</a></sup> Since 2010,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PBC-25Mar2019_30-0"><a href="#cite_note-PBC-25Mar2019-30">[30]</a></sup> China has been the world's largest single construction market.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Reuters-03Mar2011_31-0"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-03Mar2011-31">[31]</a></sup> The United States is the second largest construction market with a 2018 output of $1.581 trillion.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup>
</p><p>In the United States in February 2020, around $1.4 trillion worth of construction work was in progress, according to the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau" title="United States Census Bureau">Census Bureau</a>, of which just over $1.0 trillion was for the <a href="/wiki/Private_sector" title="Private sector">private sector</a> (split roughly 55:45% between residential and nonresidential); the remainder was <a href="/wiki/Public_sector" title="Public sector">public sector</a>, predominantly for state and local government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33"><a href="#cite_note-33">[33]</a></sup>
</p><p>Construction is a major source of employment in most countries; high reliance on small businesses, and under-representation of women are common traits. For example:
</p>
<ul><li>In the US, construction employed around 11.4m people in 2020, with a further 1.8m employed in architectural, engineering, and related professional services - equivalent to just over 8% of the total US workforce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-womenUS_34-0"><a href="#cite_note-womenUS-34">[34]</a></sup> The construction workers were employed in over 843,000 organisations, of which 838,000 were privately held businesses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USBLS-IAG_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-USBLS-IAG-35">[35]</a></sup> In March 2016, 60.4% of construction workers were employed by businesses with fewer than 50 staff.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TED-03Mar2017_36-0"><a href="#cite_note-TED-03Mar2017-36">[36]</a></sup> Women are substantially underrepresented (relative to their share of total employment), comprising 10.3% of the US construction workforce, and 25.9% of professional services workers, in 2019.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-womenUS_34-1"><a href="#cite_note-womenUS-34">[34]</a></sup></li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Construction_industry_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Construction industry of the United Kingdom">United Kingdom construction sector</a> contributed £117 billion (6%) to UK GDP in 2018, and in 2019 employed 2.4m workers (6.6% of all jobs). These worked either for 343,000 'registered' construction businesses, or for 'unregistered' businesses, typically self-employed contractors;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HoCbriefing_37-0"><a href="#cite_note-HoCbriefing-37">[37]</a></sup> just over one million small/medium-sized businesses, mainly self-employed individuals, worked in the sector in 2019, comprising about 18% of all UK businesses.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HoCbriefing2_38-0"><a href="#cite_note-HoCbriefing2-38">[38]</a></sup> Women comprised 12.5% of the UK construction workforce.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GMB_39-0"><a href="#cite_note-GMB-39">[39]</a></sup></li>
<li>In <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, the construction sector experienced growth during the latter part of 2000s. Based on National Statistical Service, Armenia's construction sector generated approximately 20% of Armenia's GDP during the first and second quarters of 2007. In 2009, according to the World Bank, 30% of Armenia's economy was from construction sector.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ArmeniaLib-Sept-20-2007_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-ArmeniaLib-Sept-20-2007-40">[40]</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>According to <a href="/wiki/McKinsey_%26_Company" title="McKinsey &amp; Company">McKinsey</a> research, <a href="/wiki/Productivity" title="Productivity">productivity</a> growth per worker in construction has lagged behind many other industries across different countries including in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and in European countries. In the United States, construction productivity per worker has declined by half since the 1960s.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Employment">Employment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Employment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Construction_Workers.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2818" data-file-width="2254" decoding="async" height="275" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Construction_Workers.jpg/220px-Construction_Workers.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Construction_Workers.jpg/330px-Construction_Workers.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Construction_Workers.jpg/440px-Construction_Workers.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Construction_Workers.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ironworker" title="Ironworker">Ironworkers</a> erecting the steel frame of a new building at <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_General_Hospital" title="Massachusetts General Hospital">Massachusetts General Hospital</a> in <a href="/wiki/Boston" title="Boston">Boston</a></div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Algamildj.jpeg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="199" data-file-width="300" decoding="async" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Algamildj.jpeg/220px-Algamildj.jpeg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/59/Algamildj.jpeg 1.5x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Algamildj.jpeg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A truck operator at <a href="/wiki/Al_Gamil" title="Al Gamil">Al Gamil</a>, the largest construction company in <a href="/wiki/Djibouti" title="Djibouti">Djibouti</a></div></div></div>
<p>Some workers may be engaged in <a href="/wiki/Manual_labour" title="Manual labour">manual labour</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43"><a href="#cite_note-43">[43]</a></sup> as unskilled or semi-skilled workers; they may be skilled tradespeople; or they may be supervisory or managerial personnel. Under safety legislation in the United Kingdom, for example, construction workers are defined as people "who work for or under the control of a contractor on a construction site";<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CDMRegs_44-0"><a href="#cite_note-CDMRegs-44">[44]</a></sup> in Canada, this can include people whose work includes ensuring conformance with building codes and regulations, and those who supervise other workers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CCOHS_45-0"><a href="#cite_note-CCOHS-45">[45]</a></sup>
</p><p><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Laborers" title="Laborers">Laborers</a> comprise a large grouping in most national construction industries. In the United States, for example, in May 2021 the construction sector employed just over 7.5 million people, of whom just over 820,000 were laborers, while 573,000 were <a href="/wiki/Carpentry" title="Carpentry">carpenters</a>, 508,000 were <a href="/wiki/Electrician" title="Electrician">electricians</a>, 258,000 were equipment operators and 230,000 were construction managers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USBLS-con_46-0"><a href="#cite_note-USBLS-con-46">[46]</a></sup> Like most business sectors, there is also substantial <a href="/wiki/White-collar_worker" title="White-collar worker">white-collar</a> employment in construction - 681,000 US workers were recorded by the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Labor" title="United States Department of Labor">United States Department of Labor</a> as in 'office and administrative support occupations' in May 2021.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USBLS-tables_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-USBLS-tables-47">[47]</a></sup>
</p><p>Large-scale construction requires <a href="/wiki/Collaboration" title="Collaboration">collaboration</a> across multiple disciplines. A <a href="/wiki/Project_manager" title="Project manager">project manager</a> normally manages the budget on the job, and a <a href="/wiki/Construction_management" title="Construction management">construction manager</a>, <a href="/wiki/Design_engineer" title="Design engineer">design engineer</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Construction_engineer" title="Construction engineer">construction engineer</a> or <a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">architect</a> supervises it. Those involved with the design and execution must consider zoning requirements and legal issues, <a href="/wiki/Environmental_impact_assessment" title="Environmental impact assessment">environmental impact</a> of the project, <a href="/wiki/Scheduling_(production_processes)" title="Scheduling (production processes)">scheduling</a>, <a href="/wiki/Budget" title="Budget">budgeting</a> and <a href="/wiki/Construction_bidding" title="Construction bidding">bidding</a>, <a href="/wiki/Construction_site_safety" title="Construction site safety">construction site safety</a>, availability and transportation of <a href="/wiki/Building_material" title="Building material">building materials</a>, logistics, and inconvenience to the public, including those caused by <a href="/wiki/Construction_delay" title="Construction delay">construction delays</a>.
</p><p>There are many routes to the different <a href="/wiki/Career" title="Career">careers</a> within the construction industry. There are three main tiers of <a href="/wiki/Construction_worker" title="Construction worker">construction workers</a> based on educational background and training, which vary by country:
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Unskilled_and_semi-skilled_workers">Unskilled and semi-skilled workers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Unskilled and semi-skilled workers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Unskilled and semi-skilled workers provide general site labor, often have few or no construction qualifications, and may receive basic site training.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Skilled_tradespeople">Skilled tradespeople</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Skilled tradespeople">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_construction_trades" title="List of construction trades">List of construction trades</a></div>
<p>Skilled <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tradesmen" title="Tradesmen">tradespeople</a> have typically served <a href="/wiki/Apprenticeship" title="Apprenticeship">apprenticeships</a> (sometimes in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Labor_union" title="Labor union">labor unions</a>) or received technical training; this group also includes on-site managers who possess extensive knowledge and experience in their <a href="/wiki/Craft" title="Craft">craft</a> or <a href="/wiki/Profession" title="Profession">profession</a>. Skilled manual occupations include <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carpenter" title="Carpenter">carpenters</a>, <a href="/wiki/Electrician" title="Electrician">electricians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Plumber" title="Plumber">plumbers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ironworker" title="Ironworker">ironworkers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heavy_equipment_operator" title="Heavy equipment operator">heavy equipment operators</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brick_mason" title="Brick mason">masons</a>, as well as those involved in project management. In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK" title="UK">UK</a> these require <a href="/wiki/Further_education" title="Further education">further education</a> qualifications, often in <a href="/wiki/Vocation" title="Vocation">vocational</a> subject areas, undertaken either directly after completing <a href="/wiki/Secondary_education" title="Secondary education">compulsory education</a> or through "on the job" apprenticeships.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="Professional.2C_technical_or_managerial_personnel"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Professional,_technical_or_managerial_personnel">Professional, technical or managerial personnel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Professional, technical or managerial personnel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Professional, technical and managerial personnel often have <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">higher education</a> qualifications, usually <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graduate_degrees" title="Graduate degrees">graduate degrees</a>, and are trained to design and manage construction processes. These roles require more training as they demand greater technical knowledge, and involve more legal responsibility. Example roles (and qualification routes) include:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Architect" title="Architect">Architect</a> – Will usually have studied <a href="/wiki/Architecture" title="Architecture">architecture</a> to degree level, and then undertaken further study and gained professional experience. In many countries, the title of "architect" is protected by law, strictly limiting its use to qualified people.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Civil_engineer" title="Civil engineer">Civil engineer</a> – Typically holds a degree in a related subject and may only be eligible for membership of a professional institution (such as the UK's <a href="/wiki/Institution_of_Civil_Engineers" title="Institution of Civil Engineers">ICE</a>) following completion of additional training and experience. In some jurisdictions, a new university graduate must hold a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master's degree">master's degree</a> to become chartered,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CEng_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-CEng-49">[a]</a></sup> and persons with <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor's degree">bachelor's degrees</a> may become <a href="/wiki/Engineering_technologist" title="Engineering technologist">Incorporated Engineers</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Building_services_engineering" title="Building services engineering">Building services engineer</a> – May also be referred to as an "M&amp;E" or "<a href="/wiki/Mechanical,_electrical,_and_plumbing" title="Mechanical, electrical, and plumbing">mechanical, electrical, and plumbing</a> (MEP) engineer" and typically holds a degree in mechanical or electrical engineering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CEng_49-1"><a href="#cite_note-CEng-49">[a]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Project_manager" title="Project manager">Project manager</a> – Typically holds a 4-year or greater <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">higher education</a> qualification, but are often also qualified in another field such as architecture, civil engineering or quantity surveying.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Structural_engineer" title="Structural engineer">Structural engineer</a> – Typically holds a bachelor's or master's degree in structural engineering.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CEng_49-2"><a href="#cite_note-CEng-49">[a]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Quantity_surveyor" title="Quantity surveyor">Quantity surveyor</a> – Typically holds a bachelor's degree in quantity surveying. UK chartered status is gained from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Institution_of_Chartered_Surveyors" title="Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors">Royal Institution of Chartered Surveyors</a>.</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Safety">Safety</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Safety">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">See also: <a href="/wiki/Construction_site_safety" title="Construction site safety">Construction site safety</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Construction_workers_not_wearing_fall_protection_equipment.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="766" data-file-width="1024" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Construction_workers_not_wearing_fall_protection_equipment.jpg/220px-Construction_workers_not_wearing_fall_protection_equipment.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Construction_workers_not_wearing_fall_protection_equipment.jpg/330px-Construction_workers_not_wearing_fall_protection_equipment.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Construction_workers_not_wearing_fall_protection_equipment.jpg/440px-Construction_workers_not_wearing_fall_protection_equipment.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Construction_workers_not_wearing_fall_protection_equipment.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>At-risk workers without appropriate safety equipment</div></div></div>
<p>Construction is one of the most dangerous occupations in the world, incurring more occupational fatalities than any other sector in both the United States and in the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OSHA_stats_4-1"><a href="#cite_note-OSHA_stats-4">[4]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eurostat_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-eurostat-50">[49]</a></sup> In the US in 2019, 1,061, or about 20%, of worker fatalities in private industry occurred in construction.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OSHA_stats_4-2"><a href="#cite_note-OSHA_stats-4">[4]</a></sup> In 2017, more than a third of US construction fatalities (366 out of 971 total fatalities) were the result of falls;<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Garza_51-0"><a href="#cite_note-Garza-51">[50]</a></sup> in the UK, half of the average 36 fatalities per annum over a five-year period to 2021 were attributed to falls from height.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HSE2021_52-0"><a href="#cite_note-HSE2021-52">[51]</a></sup> Proper safety equipment such as harnesses, hard hats and guardrails and procedures such as securing ladders and inspecting <a href="/wiki/Scaffolding" title="Scaffolding">scaffolding</a> can curtail the risk of occupational injuries in the construction industry.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-osha-campaign_53-0"><a href="#cite_note-osha-campaign-53">[52]</a></sup> Other major causes of fatalities in the construction industry include electrocution, transportation accidents, and trench cave-ins.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[53]</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Roofing-2.jpg"><img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="450" data-file-width="600" decoding="async" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Roofing-2.jpg/220px-Roofing-2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Roofing-2.jpg/330px-Roofing-2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Roofing-2.jpg/440px-Roofing-2.jpg 2x" width="220"/></a> <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Roofing-2.jpg" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Roofing requires appropriate personal protective equipment.</div></div></div>
<p>Other safety risks for workers in construction include <a href="/wiki/Hearing_loss" title="Hearing loss">hearing loss</a> due to high noise exposure, <a href="/wiki/Musculoskeletal_injury" title="Musculoskeletal injury">musculoskeletal injury</a>, chemical exposure, and high levels of stress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NIOSHblog_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-NIOSHblog-55">[54]</a></sup> Besides that, the high turnover of workers in construction industry imposes a huge challenge of accomplishing the restructuring of work practices in individual workplaces or with individual workers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>]</sup> Construction has been identified by the <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_for_Occupational_Safety_and_Health" title="National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health">National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health</a> (NIOSH) as a priority industry sector in the <a href="/wiki/National_Occupational_Research_Agenda" title="National Occupational Research Agenda">National Occupational Research Agenda</a> (NORA) to identify and provide intervention strategies regarding occupational health and safety issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[55]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[56]</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sustainability">Sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Construction&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Sustainability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096" rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style"/><div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sustainability_in_construction" title="Sustainability in construction">Sustainability in construction</a></div>
<p>Sustainability is an aspect of “green building", defined by the United States <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environmental_Protection_Agency" title="Environmental Protection Agency">Environmental Protection Agency</a> (EPA) as "the practice of creating structures and using processes that are environmentally responsible and resource-efficient throughout a building's life-cycle from siting to design, construction, operation, maintenance, renovation and deconstruction."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-:0-58">[57]</a></sup>
</p>
